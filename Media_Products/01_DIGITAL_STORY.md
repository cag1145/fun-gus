# MY FUNGAL ADVENTURE
## Digital Story — Full Screenplay

**Format:** Animated short film (3–4 minutes)
**Style:** Pixar-quality 3D animation with warm narration
**Audience:** Ages 4–9 (with adult layers)
**Media Product:** List A — Digital Story

---

## PRODUCTION NOTES

- **Aspect Ratio:** 16:9 (1920x1080)
- **Narration:** Warm female voice (think nature documentary for kids — wonder, not lecture)
- **Music:** Orchestral-organic blend. Acoustic guitar and woodwinds for forest scenes; ethereal strings and chimes for underground Sillium scenes.
- **Color Palette:** Warm greens/golds above ground → deep cyan/teal/amber underground
- **Sound Design:** Layered nature ambience throughout. Underground scenes have a low, living hum — the sound of the network breathing.

---

## COLD OPEN

**[0:00–0:15]**

**[VISUAL: Black screen. A single cyan dot pulses like a heartbeat. It sends a line of light to another dot. Then another. A web of glowing connections grows across the screen — an underground network seen from above, like a constellation in the soil.]**

**NARRATOR (V.O.):**
Beneath every forest… there's a secret.

**[The network pulses with warm amber light. The camera pulls back — we're looking down through the earth. The network stretches as far as we can see.]**

**NARRATOR (V.O.):**
A network older than dinosaurs. Bigger than the internet.

**[Beat.]**

And today, two kids are about to find it.

**[SMASH CUT to bright sunlight filtering through a canopy of leaves. TITLE CARD fades in over dappled forest light:]**

# MY FUNGAL ADVENTURE

**[Birdsong. A trail winding through an old-growth forest.]**

---

## SCENE 1: THE FOREST WALK

**[0:15–0:45]**

**[VISUAL: A sun-dappled forest trail. Late afternoon golden light cuts through oak and birch trees. Three figures walk the path: BIRCH (8, messy dark hair, yellow rain boots, purple jacket, magnifying glass on lanyard) leads the way, practically jogging. FERN (7, ponytail with a leaf stuck in it, green boots, nature journal in hand) walks carefully, looking at everything. MS. HAWTHORN (ageless, olive coat, autumn-leaf scarf, woven basket) follows behind, smiling. A robin perches on her shoulder.]**

**BIRCH:**
*(running ahead, looking through magnifying glass)*
Ms. Hawthorn! What's THAT mushroom? The one with the spots?

**MS. HAWTHORN:**
*(warmly)*
That's an amanita, Birch. Beautiful, isn't it? Fungi are some of the oldest living things on Earth.

**FERN:**
*(writing in her journal, not looking up)*
How old?

**MS. HAWTHORN:**
Older than trees. Older than flowers. They were here before almost everything else.

**[Fern stops writing. Looks up, impressed.]**

**FERN:**
That's… really old.

**MS. HAWTHORN:**
*(smiling, glancing toward something off-trail)*
And they've been busy the whole time.

**[VISUAL: Ms. Hawthorn's gaze drifts toward a clearing ahead. The robin on her shoulder tilts its head the same direction. ADULT HINT: flowers along the trail lean subtly toward her as she passes.]**

---

## SCENE 2: THE FAIRY RING

**[0:45–1:10]**

**[VISUAL: Birch pushes through a fern frond and stops dead. His magnifying glass drops to his chest.]**

**BIRCH:**
*(whispered)*
Fern. Come look at this.

**[VISUAL: A perfect circle of mushrooms in a forest clearing. Each cap glows faintly at its edges — warm white light, pulsing gently like breathing. The grass inside the ring is impossibly green. Tiny motes of golden light drift upward from the center.]**

**FERN:**
*(eyes wide)*
A fairy ring. They're real.

**BIRCH:**
And it's… glowing.

**[They look back at Ms. Hawthorn. She stands at the edge of the clearing, smiling. She does not step closer.]**

**MS. HAWTHORN:**
Go ahead. Some things you have to discover for yourselves.

**[ADULT HINT: Ms. Hawthorn smiles BEFORE the ring glows brighter — as if she expected it. She never enters the clearing.]**

**[Birch and Fern step toward the ring. Birch reaches out. Their fingers touch the edge — and the glow intensifies.]**

**BIRCH:**
Ready?

**FERN:**
*(clutching her journal)*
Ready.

**[They step inside together.]**

**[SOUND: A deep, resonant TONE — like a singing bowl crossed with a heartbeat. The golden light envelops them.]**

---

## SCENE 3: THE SHRINKING

**[1:10–1:30]**

**[VISUAL: DYNAMIC SEQUENCE. The world expands around them. Grass blades shoot up like redwoods. A pebble becomes a boulder. A beetle trundles past, now the size of a car. The color palette shifts — warm greens above give way to deep amber, then cyan-teal below. They tumble gently through layers of soil, past roots and rocks and tiny creatures, wrapped in swirling golden light.]**

**[SFX: Whooshing wind, crystalline chimes, a deep harmonic hum growing louder.]**

**FERN:**
*(laughing, falling through golden light)*
We're shrinking!

**BIRCH:**
*(delighted)*
We're REALLY shrinking!

**[VISUAL: They land softly on a bed of fine mycelium threads in an enormous underground cavern. Above them, tree roots descend like cathedral columns. In every direction, threads of mycelium branch and pulse with cyan and teal light. The space is vast, beautiful, alive.]**

**[Beat. They stare.]**

**FERN:**
*(barely a whisper)*
Where are we?

**[A warm glow approaches from the network. A friendly voice echoes through the cavern.]**

**FUN-GUS (O.S.):**
Welcome to the network!

---

## SCENE 4: MEETING FUN-GUS

**[1:30–2:00]**

**[VISUAL: FUN-GUS steps into the light. Three feet tall, warm red-orange cap with white spots, cream body, blue sash across his chest, kind glowing eyes. His gills emit a soft amber light. He walks with a gentle bounce, arms open wide.]**

**FUN-GUS:**
*(warm, enthusiastic)*
I'm Fun-Gus! And you — you found the fairy ring! Not many surface-dwellers do.

**BIRCH:**
What IS this place?

**FUN-GUS:**
*(gesturing grandly at the network around them)*
This… is Sillium. The oldest, biggest network on the planet.

**[He traces a glowing thread with his finger. Light pulses race away from his touch in both directions.]**

**FUN-GUS:**
See these threads? Each one is a conversation. Trees talking to each other. Sharing food. Sending warnings.

**FERN:**
*(scribbling furiously in her journal)*
Trees… talk?

**FUN-GUS:**
They don't use words. They use chemistry. Tiny signals traveling through threads like these — for HUNDREDS of miles in every direction.

**[VISUAL: Camera pulls back to show the vast scope of the network. Threads stretching in every direction, pulsing with light. It's beautiful — like a living galaxy underground.]**

**FUN-GUS:**
Scientists call it the "Wood Wide Web." We just call it home.

**[He turns serious for just a moment.]**

**FUN-GUS:**
But today, we have a problem.

**[He points down a thread that pulses weakly — barely glowing, flickering.]**

**FUN-GUS:**
A little seedling is losing its connection. If we don't help, it'll be cut off from the whole forest.

**[Beat.]**

Want to help?

**BIRCH:**
Yes!

**FERN:**
*(closing her journal with purpose)*
Absolutely.

---

## SCENE 5: THE TOUR — MEETING THE NETWORK

**[2:00–2:45]**

**[VISUAL: Fun-Gus leads them along the fading thread. As they walk, they meet members of the network — quick introductions, each one contributing something.]**

**[SEQUENCE A — SPORA]**

**[A tiny squeak from behind a root. SPORA floats into view — tiny, pale pink-white, enormous amber eyes, head filaments flickering like a candle in wind.]**

**SPORA:**
*(barely audible)*
I'm Spora. I'm new. I don't know how to do anything yet.

**FUN-GUS:**
*(gently)*
That's where everyone starts.

**[Spora joins the group, hovering close to Fern.]**

**[SEQUENCE B — LUMOS]**

**[The path goes dark. A trembling blue-green glow appears.]**

**LUMOS:**
*(nervous but trying)*
I'm Lumos. I light the way. I get brighter when I'm scared. Which is… a lot.

**FERN:**
Being brave doesn't mean you're not afraid.

**[Lumos glows brighter. Leads them forward. Dormant threads re-illuminate in his wake.]**

**[SEQUENCE C — PUFF]**

**[A round, rosy creature BOUNCES toward them — and BURSTS in a cloud of golden spores. Everyone is coated in sparkling dust.]**

**PUFF:**
*(ecstatic)*
Sorry! I just get so EXCITED!

**[Birch laughs. Fern sneezes. Spora peeks out from behind Fun-Gus, delighted.]**

**NARRATOR (V.O.):**
Each one different. Each one essential. And each one part of something bigger than themselves.

---

## SCENE 6: SPORA'S MOMENT

**[2:45–3:15]**

**[VISUAL: They reach the struggling seedling. Its roots are pale, barely connected. A single thread sputters with fading light. All the characters they've met are here.]**

**FUN-GUS:**
*(looking at everyone)*
This seedling needs us. All of us.

**[MONTAGE: Lumos illuminates the junction. Puff sends golden spore-signals broadcasting the call for help. Fun-Gus coordinates.]**

**[But the connection isn't complete. Everyone looks at Spora.]**

**SPORA:**
*(panicking)*
Me? But I — I'm too small. I don't know how.

**FUN-GUS:**
*(kneeling to her level)*
You don't have to be big, Spora. You just have to try.

**[Beat. Spora trembles. Her filaments flicker wildly. She reaches out one tiny tendril and touches the fading thread.]**

**[SFX: A deep, warm TONE — the same singing-bowl sound from the fairy ring.]**

**[VISUAL: Spora's filaments STABILIZE. For the first time, they hold steady — glowing warm amber-gold. A pulse of golden light races from her touch through the entire network. Every thread blazes to life. The seedling's roots fill with color — pale to warm gold. Above ground (shown in split-screen), a tiny new leaf unfurls in the sunlight.]**

**[Everyone cheers. Puff explodes again (on purpose this time). Lumos burns the brightest he's ever been.]**

**FUN-GUS:**
*(beaming)*
See? The network doesn't need you to be big. It needs you to be YOU.

**[Spora glows steady. She's found her place.]**

---

## SCENE 7: RETURN

**[3:15–3:40]**

**[VISUAL: Fun-Gus guides Birch and Fern back toward the fairy ring entrance. Golden light swirls around them again. They grow — grass shrinks back to grass, pebbles shrink back to pebbles.]**

**[They stand in the forest clearing. Same trail. Same afternoon light. The fairy ring is just ordinary mushrooms now.]**

**[Ms. Hawthorn waits on the path with her basket, smiling as if they'd only been gone a moment.]**

**MS. HAWTHORN:**
*(knowingly)*
Well? How was it?

**[Birch and Fern exchange a look.]**

**BIRCH:**
*(pressing his hand against an old oak)*
The forest was talking this whole time.

**FERN:**
*(kneeling, touching the soil beside a mushroom)*
We just couldn't hear it.

**[MS. HAWTHORN smiles. She already knew.]**

**[ADULT HINT: The robin returns to her shoulder. She hums a melody that sounds ancient and unrecognizable. She doesn't seem surprised by anything they say.]**

---

## SCENE 8: CLOSING

**[3:40–4:00]**

**[VISUAL: Wide shot — the three figures walking away down the forest trail, silhouetted against golden sunset light filtering through the canopy.]**

**[Camera slowly pushes DOWN — through the forest floor, through layers of soil, into the underground network. It's alive. Cyan threads pulse everywhere. And in the glow, tiny silhouettes: Fun-Gus waving, Spora glowing steady, Lumos lighting a path, Puff mid-burst. Connected.]**

**NARRATOR (V.O.):**
Beneath every forest, there's a network.

**[VISUAL: The network extends — filling the frame, stretching in every direction, pulsing with light.]**

**NARRATOR (V.O.):**
It's been there for 450 million years. Connecting. Sharing. Talking.

**[Beat.]**

You just have to know where to look.

**[VISUAL: TITLE CARD over the glowing network:]**

# FUN-GUS: THE FOREST HAS WIFI

**[The network pulses once more. Fade to black.]**

**[END CARD:]**

*The underground fungal networks in this story are real. Scientists call them mycorrhizae — and they connect over 90% of all plants on Earth.*

*Next time you're in a forest, remember: there's a whole internet beneath your feet.*

*Learn more at fungusbook.com*

**[CREDITS ROLL over ambient forest sounds and soft network hum.]**
