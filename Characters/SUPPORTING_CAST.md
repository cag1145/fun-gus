SUPPORTING CHARACTERS
=====================
Secondary and recurring characters in the Fun-Gus universe

---

FUNGAL CHARACTERS
=================

THE RECYCLERS
-------------
Type: Decomposer fungi (saprotrophs)
Role: Break down dead matter, return nutrients to soil
Number: A group (they work together)

Appearance:
- Smaller than Fun-Gus
- Various shapes (bracket, cup, ball)
- Warm brown/orange tones
- Busy, always in motion
- Glow shows their activity

Personality:
- Cheerful and industrious
- See beauty in their work
- Proud of their role
- Work in teams, finish each other's sentences
- "Nothing is wasted!" is their motto

Catchphrase: "One forest's ending is another's beginning!"

Teaches:
- Death is part of life's cycle
- Recycling and transformation
- Every job matters
- Teamwork

Visual Notes:
- Should look like a cleanup crew
- Happy, not morbid
- Surrounded by transformed material (dead leaf → rich soil)

---

THE LICHENS
-----------
Type: Lichen (fungus + algae symbiosis)
Role: Demonstrate ultimate partnership
Number: Usually appear as a pair (they ARE two-in-one)

Appearance:
- Crusty, textured patches
- Live on rocks and tree bark
- Two-toned coloring (fungal + algal)
- Dual glow patterns that sync

Personality:
- Always say "we," never "I"
- Finish each other's sentences
- Deeply content in their partnership
- Gentle, patient
- Have been together so long they're one

Sample Dialogue:
"We were—" "—separate once—" "—but that was—" "—so long ago—" "—we can't remember."

Teaches:
- Symbiosis at its deepest
- Two becoming one
- Partnerships strengthen both
- Identity can be shared

Visual Notes:
- Should be clearly two things that are one
- Perhaps one color/glow is more cyan (algae/photosynthesis)
- Other is more amber (fungal warmth)
- When they speak, the corresponding side glows

---

BRACKET
-------
Type: Bracket/shelf fungus (polypore)
Role: Network relay station, high observer

Appearance:
- Shelf-like, tiered brackets
- Lives on tree trunks, positioned high
- Woody texture, sturdy
- Steady amber glow (reliable)

Personality:
- Stoic and dependable
- Has watched forest for years from above
- Sees patterns others miss
- Speaks thoughtfully, slowly
- "I've seen things from up here..."

Sample Dialogue:
"The view is different from up here. You see patterns. Cycles.
That oak wasn't always the biggest. That clearing wasn't always clear."

Teaches:
- Different perspectives matter
- Patience and longevity
- Observation skills
- History is visible if you look

Visual Notes:
- Should feel like a wise lookout post
- Perhaps fungal binoculars could be a joke
- Positioned high, surveys the area
- Sturdy, unmovable, reliable

TREE CHARACTERS
===============

MOTHER OAK
----------
Type: Old-growth oak tree
Role: Elder tree, major network hub

Appearance:
- Massive above ground (implied)
- Underground: enormous root system
- Warm, deep glow
- Network threads converge on her roots

Personality:
- Nurturing, maternal
- Sends extra resources to struggling seedlings
- Calm, reassuring presence
- Speaks slowly, with great warmth
- Has seen many seasons, many changes

Voice: Deep but warm, like being hugged by sound

Sample Dialogue:
"When I was a seedling, an older oak shared with me.
Now I share with others. That is the way."

Teaches:
- Parental care exists throughout nature
- Paying it forward
- Community support
- Elders helping the young

Visual Notes:
- Root POV - her roots are like a cathedral
- Sense of warmth and safety near her
- Many smaller threads lead TO her (she's a hub)

---

THE SEEDLINGS
-------------
Type: Young tree saplings
Role: New network members, need support

Appearance:
- Tiny above ground (implied)
- Small, reaching roots below
- Flickering, unstable connection (learning)
- Nervous energy

Personality:
- Eager to grow, anxious about it
- Dependent on network for early survival
- Grateful for help
- Ask lots of questions (like Spora)

Sample Dialogue:
"I can't make enough food yet! The sun barely reaches me!"
"The network will help. That's what we're here for."

Teaches:
- Everyone starts small
- Asking for help is okay
- Community supports growth
- Patience with development

Visual Notes:
- Should evoke sympathy/nurturing response
- Similar energy to Spora (both young, learning)
- Their connection to network visibly strengthening over time

OTHER NETWORK INHABITANTS
=========================

THE SPRINGTAILS
---------------
Type: Tiny arthropods (Collembola)
Role: Spore carriers, "mail delivery"

Appearance:
- Tiny (even in shrunk world, they're small)
- Six legs, spring-loaded tail for jumping
- Covered in spore dust
- Energetic, bouncy movement

Personality:
- Hyperactive, easily distracted
- Love their job (LOVE it)
- Bounce everywhere, can't stay still
- Talk fast, move fast
- Cheerful chaos

Sample Dialogue:
"Package! Got a package! Spores to deliver! Which way? That way!
No, wait, this way! Special delivery! BOING!"

Teaches:
- Small creatures matter
- Everyone has an important role
- Energy and enthusiasm
- Sometimes the smallest do big jobs

Visual Notes:
- Comic relief potential
- Always in motion
- Covered in colorful spore dust
- Fun sound effects when they jump (boing!)

---

THE EARTHWORMS
--------------
Type: Earthworms (Lumbricidae)
Role: Tunnel makers, soil aerators

Appearance:
- Segmented, pink-brown bodies
- Gentle, undulating movement
- Leave nutrient-rich trails
- No glow (they work quietly)

Personality:
- Humble and hardworking
- Don't seek praise
- "Just doing my part"
- Steady, reliable, unglamorous
- Quietly essential

Sample Dialogue:
"Oh, don't mind me. Just making tunnels. Water needs to get through.
Air needs to get through. I just help it along. Nothing special."

Teaches:
- Unglamorous work is essential
- Humility
- Infrastructure matters
- Every role in ecosystem counts

Visual Notes:
- Should feel humble but important
- Show the tunnels they create
- Contrast to flashier characters
- Gentle, steady presence

NATURAL CHALLENGES (Not Villains)
================================

These are not characters but forces. They create conflict
without being "evil."

THE DROUGHT
-----------
What it is: Extended lack of water
Effects: Network strains, resources scarce, stress on all
Story use: Tests cooperation - who shares when there's not enough?
Lesson: Community resilience, sharing in hard times
Tone: Challenging but not hopeless

THE BLIGHT
----------
What it is: Disease spreading through network connections
Effects: Quarantine decisions, fear vs. trust, isolation
Story use: Sometimes connections carry harm - how to respond?
Lesson: Balancing openness and protection, healing
Tone: Scary but manageable with cooperation

THE DISTURBANCE
---------------
What it is: Human activity (construction, clearing, pollution)
Effects: Network damage, displacement, adaptation
Story use: Natural world responding to human impact
Lesson: Resilience, finding new paths, adaptation
Tone: NOT anti-human - humans just don't know
      Characters find ways to heal/adapt

Note on Disturbance: Humans aren't villains. They're unaware.
Part of the story's hope is that kids who learn about Sillium
will become adults who consider what's beneath their feet.

CHARACTER DESIGN PRINCIPLES
===========================

All supporting characters should:
- Have clear silhouettes
- Be visually distinct from each other
- Match the Pixar-quality aesthetic
- Have warm, approachable designs
- Serve clear narrative/educational purposes
- Be memorable even in brief appearances

Supporting cast should SUPPORT, not overshadow:
- Fun-Gus remains the guide
- Spora remains the learner
- Truff-Low remains the elder
- New characters enhance, don't replace
