### COLD OPEN

**[0:00 - 0:30]**

---

FADE IN:

EXT. UNDERGROUND -- TIMELESS

VFX: We are already underground. Total darkness. Then -- a single cyan pulse races along a glowing thread of mycelium, left to right across the screen, like a shooting star through soil. It hits a junction and SPLITS into dozens of paths, each one lighting up a new thread. The camera PULLS BACK and we see an enormous network of glowing threads stretching in every direction -- a living nervous system pulsing with light.

MUSIC: A single sustained note, low and warm, building with each new thread that illuminates. Organic. Alive.

SFX: A deep, resonant hum -- like the Earth breathing.

The camera RISES through layers of soil, past roots and rocks and tiny creatures, PUSHING UP through the dirt until --

SMASH CUT TO:

EXT. FOREST FLOOR -- DAY

SFX: Birdsong. Wind through leaves. The sudden, vivid brightness of a sunny day.

A close-up of a mushroom on the forest floor. Ordinary. Still. Sunlight dapples its cap.

MYLA (O.S.)
(calling out, excited)
Last one to the creek is a rotten log!

SFX: Footsteps running through leaves -- CRUNCH CRUNCH CRUNCH -- and two pairs of rain boots STOMP past the mushroom, barely missing it. The camera SHAKES with the impact.

The mushroom holds still. Beneath it, unseen, the faintest cyan glow pulses once and fades.

MUSIC: Theme sting -- bright, curious, adventurous.

TITLE CARD:

VFX: The title forms out of glowing mycelium threads that weave together letter by letter, pulsing with warm light:

**FUN-GUS: THE FOREST HAS WIFI**

The subtitle appears beneath, written in smaller, hand-drawn style:

**"First Contact"**

SFX: A soft, musical chime as the title completes.

### ACT ONE: THE FOREST WALK & THE FAIRY RING

**[0:35 - 2:00]**

---

EXT. TEMPERATE FOREST -- CONTINUOUS -- DAY

A sun-dappled mixed forest. Oaks, maples, birches. Shafts of golden light cut through the canopy. The forest floor is thick with ferns, mosses, and fallen leaves.

MYLA (8) races ahead on a wide trail, arms out like airplane wings. She wears bright yellow rain boots, a purple jacket, and has her hair in two messy braids. A magnifying glass bounces on a lanyard around her neck.

FERN (7) walks a few steps behind, crouched slightly, studying the ground. She wears green rain boots, a brown vest over a striped shirt, and carries a small nature journal with a pencil tucked in the spiral binding. Her hair is in a loose ponytail with leaves stuck in it that she hasn't noticed.

Behind them both, at an unhurried pace, walks MS. HAWTHORN. She is in her 40s or 50s -- it's genuinely hard to tell. She wears practical earth-toned outdoor clothing: a long olive coat, sturdy boots, a scarf the color of autumn leaves. Everything about her is warm and slightly old-fashioned. She carries a woven basket.

SFX: Forest ambiance -- birds, rustling, a distant woodpecker.

MYLA
(spinning around, walking backward to face Fern)
Fern! Fern, did you see that woodpecker? It was THIS big!

She holds her hands absurdly far apart.

FERN
(not looking up, examining something on a log)
Woodpeckers aren't that big, Myla.

MYLA
This one was.

FERN
(glancing up, small smile)
It really wasn't.

MS. HAWTHORN
(catching up, unhurried)
What have you found there, Fern?

FERN holds up a piece of bark. Tiny fungi are growing on its underside -- delicate, fan-shaped, pale orange.

FERN
These little shelves. They're growing right on the dead wood.

MS. HAWTHORN
(kneeling beside her, genuinely interested)
Now, what do you suppose they're doing there?

FERN
(thinking)
Eating it? Breaking it down?

MS. HAWTHORN
(a quiet smile, as if Fern just said something wonderful)
Very good. And what does that make the dead wood become?

FERN
(pause, then -- )
...food? For the forest?

MS. HAWTHORN
Mmm.

She doesn't say "correct." She just makes that warm sound, and it's enough.

MYLA has been bouncing impatiently during this exchange. She suddenly FREEZES. Her eyes go wide.

MYLA
Guys. GUYS.

FERN
What?

MYLA
(pointing off the trail, voice dropping to an awed whisper)
Look.

They all turn to look where Myla is pointing.

Through a gap in the trees, in a small clearing about twenty feet from the trail, there is a CIRCLE OF MUSHROOMS. A perfect ring, maybe six feet across. The mushrooms are white with pale caps, evenly spaced, growing in almost impossibly precise formation.

In the afternoon light, they seem to catch the sun differently from anything else in the forest. Not glowing -- not yet -- but luminous. As if the air above them is slightly warmer.

SFX: The forest ambiance subtly shifts. The birdsong doesn't stop, but it softens. Something in the frequency of the scene changes.

MUSIC: A delicate, curious motif. Strings and a wooden flute. Something ancient hiding inside something simple.

FERN
(opening her journal)
A fairy ring.

MYLA
A what?

FERN
A fairy ring. It's when mushrooms grow in a circle. I read about them.

MYLA
(already walking toward it)
It's PERFECT. Like someone drew it with a compass.

FERN
(following, but slower)
Wait -- I want to draw it first --

MS. HAWTHORN stands at the edge of the trail. She has not moved. A small, private expression crosses her face -- not surprise, but recognition. As if she's been waiting for this moment. She looks at the ring, then at the girls, then back at the ring.

VFX: SUBTLE -- for adult viewers only. As Ms. Hawthorn looks at the fairy ring, the mushrooms seem to glow just slightly brighter. Just for a moment. Then it passes.

A robin lands on a branch near Ms. Hawthorn's shoulder. It doesn't fly away. She doesn't seem to notice, as if this is perfectly normal.

MS. HAWTHORN
(following the girls, softly, more to herself than to them)
Ah. There it is.

The girls reach the ring. They stand at its edge, looking in.

MYLA
(crouching down, peering at a mushroom)
They're so clean. Like little tables.

FERN
(sketching rapidly in her journal)
The spacing is really regular. Every one is the same distance from the next.

MYLA
Why though?

FERN
(pausing her sketch, thinking)
I... don't know.

MS. HAWTHORN arrives behind them.

MS. HAWTHORN
In the old stories, they said fairy rings were doorways.

MYLA
(eyes lighting up)
Doorways to WHERE?

MS. HAWTHORN
(with a knowing look the girls don't catch)
To wherever you're brave enough to step.

MYLA
(to Fern, grabbing her hand)
Come on!

FERN
(pulling back slightly)
Myla, wait --

MYLA
(not letting go, grinning)
Together?

A beat. Fern looks at the ring. She looks at Myla. She closes her journal and tucks it under her arm.

FERN
(deep breath)
Together.

They step into the ring. Both feet inside the circle.

For one second, nothing happens. Fern exhales, relieved, almost disappointed.

Then --

SFX: A deep, resonant TONE -- like the Earth exhaling. Not loud. Felt more than heard.

VFX: The mushrooms around the ring begin to GLOW. Not all at once -- one by one, like lights on a runway, each cap illuminating with soft warm white light. The glow rises from the base of each mushroom, climbing upward. When the last mushroom lights, the glow connects -- a continuous circle of light around the girls' feet.

FERN
(grabbing Myla's arm)
Myla --

MYLA
(breathless, delighted)
I SEE IT.

The light intensifies. The ground beneath their feet begins to shimmer. VFX: Tiny particles of golden light rise from the soil like inverted snow, drifting upward around the girls.

MUSIC: The motif swells. Strings joined by a choir of humming voices -- organic, ancient, beautiful.

Ms. Hawthorn watches from outside the ring. She does not look alarmed. She looks like a parent watching a child take their first steps.

MS. HAWTHORN
(quietly, warmly)
There you go.

VFX: The golden particles swirl faster. The girls' perspective begins to SHIFT -- the trees around the clearing seem to GROW, stretching taller and taller. The mushrooms at the edge of the ring rise like towers. Fern's nature journal slips from under her arm and falls -- but it falls UP, away from them, growing enormous as it tumbles.

SFX: A cascading shimmer -- like wind chimes made of crystal, descending in pitch as the girls shrink.

They are SHRINKING. Rapidly but gently, like falling in slow motion. The world expands around them. Blades of grass become trees. Grains of soil become boulders. A beetle trundles past like a bus.

The girls grip each other's hands.

MYLA
(screaming with joy, not fear)
WHOOOOAAAA!

FERN
(screaming, also not entirely fear)
WHAT'S HAPPENING?!

---

### ACT TWO: THE SHRINKING & THE UNDERGROUND REVEAL

**[2:00 - 3:30]**

---

EXT./INT. TRANSITION -- FOREST FLOOR TO UNDERGROUND -- CONTINUOUS

VFX: The girls pass THROUGH the surface of the soil. This is the transition moment. They fall gently through layers: past roots like wooden highways, past sleeping beetles, past pebbles the size of houses. The light changes from golden daylight to deep amber, then to rich earth-tones, then --

Darkness. For one breath. Silence.

SFX: Dripping water, echoing. The sound of a vast underground space.

Then --

VFX: THE REVEAL.

A single thread of mycelium ignites with CYAN light directly in front of them. It stretches away into the distance. Then another. Then another. Like stars appearing at dusk, the threads illuminate one by one, and the space around the girls is revealed:

They are standing in an ENORMOUS underground chamber. The ceiling arcs far above them, supported by root systems that descend like the columns of a cathedral. Every surface is alive -- coated in a filigree of glowing mycelium threads, pulsing with gentle cyan and teal light. The threads branch and reconnect, forming patterns that are almost -- but not quite -- geometric. Organic circuitry.

Pulses of light travel along the threads -- some fast, some slow, some in clusters. They are MESSAGES. They hum faintly as they pass.

This is the ENTRY CHAMBER of SILLIUM.

MUSIC: The full theme arrives. Orchestral but organic -- woodwinds and strings woven with synthesized tones that sound alive. Awe. Pure, uncomplicated awe.

SFX: The ambient hum of the network -- a warm, low chord made of a thousand tiny sounds. Not mechanical. Biological.

The girls stand in the middle of this, still holding hands, mouths open.

A long beat. Let the audience absorb it.

FERN
(barely a whisper)
Oh...

MYLA
(same)
...wow.

Fern slowly lets go of Myla's hand. She reaches out and touches a nearby mycelium thread. It pulses AMBER-GOLD at her touch, and the pulse ripples outward along the thread like a stone dropped in water.

FERN
(pulling her hand back)
It felt my hand. It -- it responded.

MYLA
(reaching for a thread herself)
Let me try!

She touches one. It pulses AMBER-GOLD, and the pulse shoots away down the thread, faster than Fern's.

MYLA
(grinning)
Mine was faster!

FERN
(small smile)
Mine was more careful.

SFX: A gentle rustling from deeper in the chamber. Something is approaching.

The girls turn toward the sound. The mycelium threads in that direction glow slightly brighter with WARM WHITE light, as if illuminating a path. The light travels toward them, getting closer.

MYLA
(stepping in front of Fern, instinctively protective)
Something's coming.

FERN
(peering around Myla's shoulder)
Look at the light. It's... warm. Not warning.

VFX: The warm white glow intensifies and then, stepping out from behind a massive root column --

FUN-GUS appears.

He is a three-foot-tall AMANITA MUSHROOM -- red-orange cap with white spots, cream-colored body, a warm and open face with large expressive eyes. He wears a blue sash across his body, and on it sits a small golden device that interfaces with the Sillium network -- his gills glow softly with warm white light. He moves with a gentle, bouncing walk, and his entire presence radiates welcome.

He sees the girls and his face LIGHTS UP.

FUN-GUS
(spreading his arms wide, warm and joyful)
Well, well, WELL! Visitors! Oh, this is -- this is WONDERFUL!

---

### ACT THREE: MEETING FUN-GUS & SPORA

**[3:30 - 4:45]**

---

INT. SILLIUM -- ENTRY CHAMBER -- CONTINUOUS

FUN-GUS bounces toward the girls, practically vibrating with excitement. The mycelium threads around him pulse in rhythm with his steps.

FUN-GUS
(bowing slightly, cap tilting forward)
Welcome, welcome, welcome to Sillium! I'm Fun-Gus, your friendly neighborhood network guide!

MYLA
(amazed, no fear at all)
You're a MUSHROOM. A TALKING mushroom.

FUN-GUS
(chuckling)
Well, technically, THIS --
(gestures to himself)
-- is only a small part of me. I'm like an iceberg! What you see is just the tip. Most of me is down here.

He gestures to the vast web of mycelium threads stretching in every direction.

FUN-GUS (CONT'D)
All of THAT is me too. And my family. And my neighbors. All connected.

FERN
(eyes wide, putting it together)
The mushrooms on the surface -- the ones we see in the forest -- they're just... the top part?

FUN-GUS
(pointing at Fern, delighted)
YES! Oh, you're quick! Imagine if you could only see the chimneys of houses. You'd never know about the whole building underneath, would you?

VFX: As Fun-Gus speaks, a VISUAL DIAGRAM briefly illuminates around them in the mycelium -- a mushroom cross-section showing the cap above ground, then the VAST network of threads below. It forms naturally in the glowing threads, then fades.

FERN
(to Myla, awed)
The mushrooms are like... periscopes. And this whole network is the submarine.

MYLA
(looking around, spinning slowly)
So what IS all this? What does it DO?

FUN-GUS
(rubbing his hands together, this is his favorite topic)
Oh, oh, oh. You want to know what Sillium does?

He walks between them and puts a stubby arm around each of their shoulders, turning them to face the vast chamber and its pulsing threads.

FUN-GUS (CONT'D)
See those pulses? Those lights moving through the threads?

They nod. Cyan and teal pulses race along hundreds of threads in every direction.

FUN-GUS (CONT'D)
Those are messages. Trees talking to trees. Fungi talking to fungi. Everybody sharing information, sharing food, sharing warnings.

(beat, then with a conspiratorial grin)

Think of it like... the world's oldest group chat.

MYLA
(jaw dropping)
TREES can TALK?!

FUN-GUS
Not with words, exactly. With chemistry! A tree gets munched on by a caterpillar, and it sends a signal through my network --
(traces a path along a thread with his finger)
-- all the way to the other trees, saying "Heads up! Bug attack! Make your leaves taste bad!"

VFX: As he describes it, a RED-ORANGE warning pulse races along the thread he's tracing, branching out to multiple endpoints. At each endpoint, a faint outline of a tree briefly illuminates.

FERN
(fascinated)
Like an alarm system.

FUN-GUS
Like an alarm system, a postal service, a restaurant delivery app, and a library, all rolled into one. And it's been running for --

He pauses for effect.

FUN-GUS (CONT'D)
-- four hundred and fifty MILLION years.

MYLA
(turning to Fern)
That's before DINOSAURS!

FUN-GUS
(proudly)
WAY before dinosaurs.

SFX: A tiny, uncertain sound from behind a root nearby. A squeak, almost. Something small and shy.

Fun-Gus hears it. His expression softens immediately.

FUN-GUS
(lowering his voice, turning toward the sound)
Ah. Speaking of the network...

He crouches down and gently calls toward a cluster of root fibers.

FUN-GUS (CONT'D)
(softly)
It's all right. You can come out. We have guests.

A tiny pause. Then, slowly, nervously, SPORA emerges.

She is TINY -- even at their already-shrunken scale, she is small. A round, pale pink-white body with enormous amber eyes. Delicate neural filaments extend from the top of her head, but they flicker and sputter -- unstable, like a lightbulb with a loose connection. She floats very slightly off the ground, bobbing.

She peeks at the girls from behind Fun-Gus's leg.

SPORA
(tiny, uncertain voice)
H-hello?

MYLA
(melting, crouching down to Spora's level)
Oh my gosh. Hi!

FERN
(also crouching, speaking gently)
Hey there. What's your name?

SPORA
(stepping out slightly, still half-hidden)
I'm... Spora. I'm new.

FUN-GUS
(placing a gentle hand on Spora's head)
Spora here is one of our newest spores. She's still learning how the network works. Just like you two!

SPORA
(looking up at Fun-Gus, then at the girls)
Am I... am I doing it right?

Her head filaments flicker uncertainly. One of them sputters out entirely. She looks embarrassed.

FERN
(immediately, warmly)
I don't think there IS a wrong way. You're here. That's the first step.

Spora looks at Fern. Her filaments flicker slightly brighter. Not stable yet -- but a little less dim.

SPORA
(small smile)
Really?

MYLA
(standing up, looking at Fun-Gus)
Can she come with us? On the tour?

FUN-GUS
(beaming)
I was hoping you'd ask.

Spora floats up to eye level with the girls, still bobbing uncertainly but no longer hiding.

### ACT FOUR: THE THIRSTY SEEDLING

**[4:45 - 6:00]**

---

INT. SILLIUM -- THE MAIN NETWORK -- CONTINUOUS

Fun-Gus leads the group through a wider tunnel of mycelium. The walls are alive with threading light. Spora floats beside Fern, occasionally reaching out a tiny tendril to touch a passing thread. Each time she does, her filaments glow briefly AMBER-GOLD, then fade.

SPORA
(trying again, filaments flickering)
I want to send a message, but I can't make it go all the way...

FERN
(watching closely)
Your light is really pretty when you try, though.

SPORA
(small, pleased sound)
...you think so?

FUN-GUS
(walking ahead, turns back)
Sending messages takes practice, Spora. You'll --

He stops. Holds up a hand. The group halts.

FUN-GUS (CONT'D)
(tilting his head, listening)
Hmm. Do you feel that?

They all go still. The ambient hum of the network changes -- somewhere ahead, there is a gap. A dull spot. The threads still glow, but along one particular line, the pulses are STUTTERING. Weak. Thinning out.

MYLA
One of the lights is going dim.

FERN
(pointing)
That one. See? The pulses aren't getting through.

Fun-Gus walks toward the weak thread and follows it. The group follows him into a smaller chamber where ROOTS descend from above -- thick, ancient roots from a massive tree, and beside them, one set of roots that are TINY. Thin. Fragile.

VFX: The tiny roots have almost no glow. They're reaching downward, but they look parched. Dry. The mycelium threads connect to them, but the connection is faint.

FUN-GUS
(kneeling beside the small roots, concerned)
Oh, little one. What's happened to you?

He places his hand on the connection point between the mycelium and the tiny roots.

FUN-GUS (CONT'D)
This is a seedling. A baby tree, just started growing up above. But it's not getting enough water. Its roots are too small to reach the deep moisture on their own.

MYLA
Can't you just... send it water?

FUN-GUS
(looking at her, then at the network around them)
That's exactly what the network is for. But this connection --
(indicating the faint thread linking the seedling to the larger root system)
-- it's weak. The seedling only just joined. It hasn't built strong links yet.

FERN
(quiet realization)
It's new. Like Spora.

Spora and Fern exchange a look. Spora's filaments flicker.

FUN-GUS
Here's the thing about Sillium. No one survives alone down here. That big tree up there --
(pointing to the massive roots above)
-- that's Mother Oak. She's got more water and sugar than she needs. And this little seedling is right next door. The network WANTS to help. But the connection has to be strong enough to carry the signal.

MYLA
So we need to boost the signal?

FUN-GUS
(smiling)
We need to boost the signal.

Fun-Gus places both hands on the mycelium thread connecting the seedling to the larger network. His gills glow WARM WHITE. A pulse of light travels down the thread -- but it fades before reaching the main network.

FUN-GUS
(concentrating)
Not quite enough. I need a little help.

He looks at the girls. Then at Spora.

MYLA
(immediately putting her hands on the thread)
I'm in!

Fern does the same, placing her hands carefully on the thread.

Fun-Gus looks at Spora.

FUN-GUS
(gently)
What do you say, little one?

SPORA
(looking at the dim seedling, then at her own flickering filaments)
But... I can barely send a message. What if I'm not strong enough?

FERN
(looking at Spora)
You don't have to be strong enough alone. That's literally what the network is for.

A beat. Spora floats down to the thread. She extends her tiny tendrils and touches it.

FUN-GUS
Now -- together.

MUSIC: A building swell. Each character adds their energy to the thread.

VFX: Fun-Gus glows WARM WHITE. The girls' hands pulse AMBER-GOLD. And Spora -- her filaments flicker, stutter, flicker again --

And then they HOLD.

Her filaments stabilize into a steady, bright AMBER-GOLD glow. For the first time, the light is constant.

The combined pulse races down the thread -- BRIGHT, strong, unmistakable -- and hits the main network. It branches. It reaches Mother Oak's roots.

SFX: A deep, warm resonance from above. The sound of something VAST waking up.

VFX: Mother Oak's roots illuminate with rich CYAN-TEAL light. A pulse returns -- heavy, slow, generous -- carrying water and nutrients back along the thread toward the seedling.

The pulse reaches the seedling's roots. They GLOW. The thin, parched roots fill with light, plumping slightly, brightening from dull brown to warm gold.

MOTHER OAK (V.O.)
(a deep, resonant, maternal voice from the roots above -- not a character they see, but a presence they FEEL)
Drink, little one. You are not alone.

The seedling's roots extend just a little further into the soil. A tiny new tendril unfurls.

MYLA
(fist pump)
YES!

FERN
(quiet wonder)
Mother Oak shared her water. Through the network. Through US.

FUN-GUS
(putting his arms around both girls, emotional)
Through ALL of us. That's what Sillium does. No one out here is just looking out for themselves. The big trees feed the little ones. The fungi carry the messages. And sometimes --
(looking at Spora)
-- the newest voice in the network is exactly the one that was needed.

Spora's filaments glow steadily. She stares at them, amazed.

SPORA
(wonderstruck)
They're not flickering anymore.

FUN-GUS
(warm)
You made your first real connection, Spora. The network heard you.

From deep in the chamber, beyond the roots, in a place where the light is DEEP GREEN and very old, something stirs. A low rumble. Not threatening -- ancient.

VFX: A pair of orange cracks glow in the deep darkness. The mossy surface of something MASSIVE briefly illuminates. TRUFF-LOW. We can barely see him -- just the outline of an enormous truffle, half-buried, covered in moss, ancient beyond measure. His orange cracks pulse once, slowly.

TRUFF-LOW
(deep, slow, a voice like stones settling)
Listen.

Everyone goes quiet.

TRUFF-LOW (CONT'D)
(each word deliberate, weighted with millennia)
The network... remembers. Every drop shared. Every signal sent. For... four hundred million years.

His orange glow pulses once more, then dims. He settles back into silence.

A beat. The group stands in the quiet that follows.

FUN-GUS
(softly, reverently)
That's Truff-Low. He doesn't speak often. But when he does...

FERN
(finishing the thought, whisper)
...you listen.

### ACT FIVE: RETURN TO THE SURFACE

**[6:00 - 6:50]**

---

INT. SILLIUM -- ENTRY CHAMBER -- MOMENTS LATER

Fun-Gus leads the group back through the tunnels to the Entry Chamber. The network hums around them, alive and warm. Spora floats beside the girls, her filaments glowing steady amber-gold.

FUN-GUS
(stopping at a point where a column of light rises upward through the soil)
Here we are. The way back up.

VFX: A vertical shaft of golden light, rising from the fairy ring above. Tiny particles drift downward through it like motes of dust in a sunbeam -- but these particles glow.

MYLA
(her enthusiasm dropping for the first time)
Already?

FUN-GUS
(chuckling)
Time has a funny habit of slipping away down here. But the ring will always be there. When you're ready to come back...

MYLA
(immediately)
Tomorrow.

FUN-GUS
(laughing)
...you'll know where to find us.

FERN
(kneeling down to Spora)
Will you be okay?

SPORA
(floating up to face Fern, filaments glowing steady)
I think so. I have the network now.

(pause)

Will YOU be okay? Up there, without it?

FERN
(touched, smiling)
I think I'll be okay. Knowing it's here.

MYLA
(high-fiving Spora -- Spora doesn't quite understand the gesture but slaps Myla's finger with a tiny tendril)
See you soon, Spora!

SPORA
(giggling)
See you soon!

FUN-GUS
(stepping back, placing his hand over his chest and bowing his cap)
Until next time, friends. Remember -- the forest has WiFi. You just have to know where to look.

The girls step into the column of light.

VFX: The golden particles swirl upward around them. The world compresses. The underground chamber recedes below as they RISE through the layers of soil -- past roots and rocks and tiny creatures -- getting LARGER, the world getting smaller, until --

---

EXT. FOREST -- THE FAIRY RING -- LATE AFTERNOON

SFX: Birdsong rushes back. Wind in the leaves.

The girls are standing in the fairy ring. Full size. The mushrooms around them are no longer glowing -- just ordinary white caps in a circle. The forest is exactly as they left it. Late afternoon sun cuts through the trees.

For a moment, the girls just stand there. Breathing. Looking at each other.

MYLA
(quietly, for once)
Did that just...

FERN
(nodding slowly)
Yeah.

They step out of the ring.

Ms. Hawthorn is sitting on a fallen log nearby, her woven basket beside her. She looks up as if they've only been gone for a moment -- no concern, no surprise, no questions about where they went.

MS. HAWTHORN
(smiling, as if she already knows)
Well? How was the forest today?

MYLA
(looking at Fern, then back at Ms. Hawthorn, bursting)
Ms. Hawthorn, did you KNOW about -- I mean, have you ever -- under the --

She stops. She doesn't have the words yet.

MS. HAWTHORN

About what, dear?

MYLA

MS. HAWTHORN
(that knowing smile again)
What about them?

FERN
(quietly, looking at Ms. Hawthorn closely)
You knew. Didn't you? You knew what the ring could do.

Ms. Hawthorn holds Fern's gaze for a beat. Something passes between them -- not quite an answer, not quite a denial.

MS. HAWTHORN
(standing, brushing off her coat)
I know that the forest has a great many things to show, to those who step carefully and pay attention.

(she picks up her basket)

Shall we head back? I believe it's nearly suppertime.

She starts walking back toward the trail. The robin from earlier lands on a branch beside her, chirps twice, and flies ahead as if scouting the path.

VFX: SUBTLE -- as Ms. Hawthorn passes the fairy ring, the mushrooms glow -- just for a heartbeat, just the faintest warm white -- and then go still.

The girls watch her walk, then look at each other.

MYLA
(whispering)
She totally knew.

FERN
(whispering back)
How?

They don't have an answer. They follow Ms. Hawthorn down the trail.

As they walk, FERN reaches out and places her hand on the trunk of a large oak tree. She closes her eyes, just for a moment.

MUSIC: The network motif plays softly -- one last time. Warm. Connected.

VFX: From Fern's perspective -- a BRIEF FLASH of the underground. Not a fantasy, not a daydream -- a memory. The glowing threads. The seedling drinking. Spora's filaments going steady. All of it, right below her feet, right now.

She opens her eyes and smiles.

FERN
(to the tree, quiet, private)
I know you're in there.

She runs to catch up with Myla and Ms. Hawthorn.

### Educational Beats Delivered

| Beat | Where It Appears | Method |
|------|------------------|--------|
| Mushrooms are just the visible part | Fun-Gus's iceberg analogy + chimney analogy | Dialogue + visual diagram in mycelium |
| Trees and fungi are connected underground | Fun-Gus's group chat explanation | Analogy + VFX showing message paths |
| They share food and water | The Thirsty Seedling sequence | Active problem-solving, not lecture |

### Ms. Hawthorn Faerie Hints (for Adult Viewers)

| Hint | Moment |
|------|--------|
| She knows exactly where the fairy ring is | "Ah. There it is." -- not searching, recognizing |
| The ring glows brighter near her | VFX when she looks at ring; VFX as she passes it at end |
| Animals respond to her | Robin lands on branch near her, doesn't fly away; robin scouts her path at end |
| She's never surprised by anything magical | Zero alarm when girls vanish; "How was the forest?" as if nothing unusual happened |
| Old-fashioned speech patterns | "Now, what do you suppose..." / "I believe it's nearly suppertime" |
| She asks questions instead of giving answers | Never directly explains anything; leads children to discover |
| She may have done this before | Her calm, practiced demeanor; "to those who step carefully and pay attention" |

### Pacing Notes for Storyboard Artists

- **Cold open (0:00-0:30):** FAST. Underground pulse, smash cut to surface, boots stomping past mushroom. Grab attention with contrast: vast alien network vs. familiar forest.
- **Act One (0:35-2:00):** Build slowly. Character establishment through natural dialogue. Let the fairy ring discovery land with WEIGHT -- slow the pace when they see it. The shrinking sequence accelerates.
- **Act Two (2:00-3:30):** BREATHE. The underground reveal needs TIME. Hold on wide shots. Let the audience absorb the scale. The hum should fill the space. Fun-Gus's entrance should break the reverence with warmth.
- **Act Three (3:30-4:45):** Conversational pace. Education lives here but delivered through enthusiasm, not lecture. Spora's introduction should soften the energy -- she's small and uncertain.
- **Act Four (4:45-6:00):** Rising action. The seedling problem creates urgency. The solution should feel EARNED -- Spora's moment of stability is the emotional peak. Truff-Low's appearance is the awe peak. Two different kinds of climax.
- **Act Five (6:00-6:50):** Denouement. Warm, reflective. The Ms. Hawthorn scene should feel like a quiet mystery. Fern touching the tree is the emotional landing. Don't rush it.

### Sound Design Direction

- **Above Ground:** Natural, warm, familiar. Birdsong, wind, footsteps on leaves. Comfortable.
- **The Transition:** The natural sounds don't cut out -- they TRANSFORM. Birdsong becomes the hum. Wind becomes the pulse. The familiar world BECOMES the network.
- **Underground:** Organic ambiance. Not silence, not noise -- PRESENCE. The network hums like a living thing. Pulses have soft tonal qualities. Different thread colors could have subtly different pitches.
- **Mother Oak's Voice:** Felt as much as heard. Low frequency. Resonant. Like standing inside a cello.
- **Truff-Low:** His voice should vibrate the space. Not loud -- deep. The kind of sound that makes you still.

### Visual Style Key Frames Needed

1. **The Mushroom Close-Up (Cold Open)** -- A single mushroom on the forest floor. Ordinary above. Glowing below. The duality of the series in one image.
2. **The Fairy Ring Discovery** -- The clearing, the perfect circle, the luminous quality of the air. Wonder before magic.
3. **The Shrinking** -- Mid-shrink POV. A blade of grass becoming a tree. Scale shift as emotion.
4. **The Underground Reveal** -- The cathedral of mycelium. The WOW shot. This sells the series.
5. **Fun-Gus's Entrance** -- Warm white glow emerging from behind a root. Friendly. Inviting. Iconic.
6. **Spora Peeking Out** -- Tiny, uncertain, enormous eyes. The audience falls in love here.
7. **The Seedling Rescue -- All Hands In** -- Four sets of hands/tendrils on the glowing thread. Cooperation visualized.
8. **Spora's Filaments Stabilize** -- The flickering stops. Steady amber-gold. Growth. Belonging.
9. **Truff-Low in the Deep** -- Barely visible. Ancient. Orange cracks glowing. Mystery and gravitas.
10. **Fern Touching the Oak** -- A girl's hand on bark. Eyes closed. Knowing what's underneath. The image that says "the world is more than you can see."
