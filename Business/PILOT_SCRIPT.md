### COLD OPEN

**[0:00 - 0:45]**

---

FADE IN:

EXT. UNDERGROUND -- TIMELESS

VFX: Total darkness. Then -- a single cyan pulse races along a glowing thread of mycelium, left to right across the screen, like a shooting star through soil. It hits a junction and SPLITS into dozens of paths, each one lighting up a new thread. The camera PULLS BACK and we see an enormous network of glowing threads stretching in every direction -- a living nervous system pulsing with light.

MUSIC: A single sustained note, low and warm, building with each new thread that illuminates. Organic. Alive.

SFX: A deep, resonant hum -- like the Earth breathing.

The camera RISES through layers of soil, past roots and rocks and tiny creatures, PUSHING UP through the dirt until --

SMASH CUT TO:

EXT. FOREST FLOOR -- DAY

SFX: Birdsong. Wind through leaves. The sudden, vivid brightness of a sunny day.

A close-up of a mushroom on the forest floor. Ordinary. Still. Sunlight dapples its cap.

BIRCH (O.S.)
(calling out, excited)
Last one to the creek is a rotten log!

SFX: Footsteps running through leaves -- CRUNCH CRUNCH CRUNCH -- and two pairs of rain boots STOMP past the mushroom, barely missing it. The camera SHAKES with the impact.

The mushroom holds still. Beneath it, unseen, the faintest cyan glow pulses once and fades.

But then -- somewhere far below, along one of those glowing threads -- a single pulse STUTTERS. Dims. Flickers like a dying heartbeat.

Something in the network is struggling.

MUSIC: Theme sting -- bright, curious, adventurous -- but with one note slightly off. A question mark.

TITLE CARD:

VFX: The title forms out of glowing mycelium threads that weave together letter by letter, pulsing with warm light:

**FUN-GUS: THE FOREST HAS WIFI**

The subtitle appears beneath, written in smaller, hand-drawn style:

**"First Contact"**

SFX: A soft, musical chime as the title completes.

### ACT ONE: INTO SILLIUM

**[0:50 - 5:00]**

---

#### SCENE 1: THE FOREST WALK

**[0:50 - 1:45]**

EXT. TEMPERATE FOREST -- DAY

A sun-dappled mixed forest. Oaks, maples, birches. Shafts of golden light cut through the canopy. The forest floor is thick with ferns, mosses, and fallen leaves.

BIRCH (8) races ahead on a wide trail, arms out like airplane wings. He wears bright yellow rain boots, a purple jacket, and has short messy dark hair. A magnifying glass bounces on a lanyard around his neck.

FERN (7) walks a few steps behind, crouched slightly, studying the ground. She wears green rain boots, a brown vest over a striped shirt, and carries a small nature journal with a pencil tucked in the spiral binding. Her hair is in a loose ponytail with leaves stuck in it that she hasn't noticed.

Behind them both, at an unhurried pace, walks MS. HAWTHORN. She wears practical earth-toned outdoor clothing: a long olive coat, sturdy boots, a scarf the color of autumn leaves. Everything about her is warm and slightly old-fashioned. She carries a woven basket.

SFX: Forest ambiance -- birds, rustling, a distant woodpecker.

BIRCH
(spinning around, walking backward)
Fern! Did you see that woodpecker? It was THIS big!

He holds his hands absurdly far apart.

FERN
(not looking up, examining something on a log)
Woodpeckers aren't that big, Birch.

BIRCH
This one was.

FERN
(glancing up, small smile)
It really wasn't.

Fern holds up a piece of bark. Tiny fungi are growing on its underside -- delicate, fan-shaped, pale orange.

FERN (CONT'D)
These little shelves. They're growing right on the dead wood.

MS. HAWTHORN
(kneeling beside her, genuinely interested)
Now, what do you suppose they're doing there?

FERN
(thinking)
Eating it? Breaking it down?

MS. HAWTHORN
(a quiet smile, as if Fern just said something wonderful)
Very good. And what does that make the dead wood become?

FERN
...food? For the forest?

MS. HAWTHORN
Mmm.

She doesn't say "correct." She just makes that warm sound, and it's enough.

---

#### SCENE 2: THE FAIRY RING

**[1:45 - 2:45]**

Birch suddenly FREEZES. His eyes go wide.

BIRCH
Guys. GUYS.

Through a gap in the trees, in a small clearing about twenty feet from the trail, there is a CIRCLE OF MUSHROOMS. A perfect ring, maybe six feet across. The mushrooms are white with pale caps, evenly spaced, growing in almost impossibly precise formation.

In the afternoon light, they seem to catch the sun differently from anything else in the forest. Not glowing -- not yet -- but luminous. As if the air above them is slightly warmer.

SFX: The forest ambiance subtly shifts. The birdsong softens. Something in the frequency of the scene changes.

MUSIC: A delicate, curious motif. Strings and a wooden flute. Something ancient hiding inside something simple.

FERN
(opening her journal)
A fairy ring.

BIRCH
(already walking toward it)
It's PERFECT. Like someone drew it with a compass.

MS. HAWTHORN stands at the edge of the trail. A small, private expression crosses her face -- not surprise, but recognition. As if she's been waiting for this moment.

VFX: SUBTLE -- for adult viewers only. As Ms. Hawthorn looks at the fairy ring, the mushrooms glow just slightly brighter. Just for a moment.

A robin lands on a branch near Ms. Hawthorn's shoulder. It doesn't fly away. She doesn't seem to notice, as if this is perfectly normal.

MS. HAWTHORN
(following the kids, softly)
In the old stories, they said fairy rings were doorways.

BIRCH
(eyes lighting up)
Doorways to WHERE?

MS. HAWTHORN
(with a knowing look the kids don't catch)
To wherever you're brave enough to step.

BIRCH
(grabbing Fern's hand)
Come on!

FERN
(pulling back slightly)
Birch, wait --

BIRCH
Together?

A beat. Fern looks at the ring. She looks at Birch. She closes her journal and tucks it under her arm.

FERN
(deep breath)
Together.

They step into the ring.

---

#### SCENE 3: THE SHRINKING

**[2:45 - 3:30]**

SFX: A deep, resonant TONE -- like the Earth exhaling. Not loud. Felt more than heard.

VFX: The mushrooms around the ring begin to GLOW. One by one, like lights on a runway, each cap illuminating with soft warm white light. When the last mushroom lights, the glow connects -- a continuous circle of light around the kids' feet.

The light intensifies. Tiny particles of golden light rise from the soil like inverted snow, drifting upward around the kids.

MUSIC: The motif swells. Strings joined by a choir of humming voices -- organic, ancient, beautiful.

Ms. Hawthorn watches from outside the ring. She does not look alarmed. She looks like a parent watching a child take their first steps.

MS. HAWTHORN
(quietly, warmly)
There you go.

VFX: The golden particles swirl faster. The trees around the clearing seem to GROW, stretching taller and taller. Fern's nature journal slips from under her arm and falls -- but it falls UP, growing enormous as it tumbles.

They are SHRINKING. Rapidly but gently, like falling in slow motion. The world expands around them. Blades of grass become trees. Grains of soil become boulders. A beetle trundles past like a bus.

BIRCH
(screaming with joy)
WHOOOOAAAA!

FERN
(screaming, not entirely fear)
WHAT'S HAPPENING?!

VFX: The kids pass THROUGH the surface of the soil. They fall gently through layers: past roots like wooden highways, past sleeping beetles, past pebbles the size of houses. The light changes from golden daylight to deep amber, then to rich earth-tones, then --

Darkness. For one breath. Silence.

SFX: Dripping water, echoing. The sound of a vast underground space.

---

#### SCENE 4: THE UNDERGROUND REVEAL & MEETING FUN-GUS

**[3:30 - 5:00]**

Then --

VFX: THE REVEAL.

A single thread of mycelium ignites with CYAN light directly in front of them. It stretches away into the distance. Then another. Then another. Like stars appearing at dusk, the threads illuminate one by one, and the space around the kids is revealed:

They are standing in an ENORMOUS underground chamber. The ceiling arcs far above them, supported by root systems that descend like the columns of a cathedral. Every surface is alive -- coated in a filigree of glowing mycelium threads, pulsing with gentle cyan and teal light. The threads branch and reconnect, forming organic circuitry.

Pulses of light travel along the threads -- some fast, some slow, some in clusters. They are MESSAGES. They hum faintly as they pass.

This is the ENTRY CHAMBER of SILLIUM.

MUSIC: The full theme arrives. Orchestral but organic -- woodwinds and strings woven with synthesized tones that sound alive. Awe. Pure, uncomplicated awe.

The kids stand in the middle of this, still holding hands, mouths open.

A long beat. Let the audience absorb it.

FERN
(barely a whisper)
Oh...

BIRCH
(same)
...wow.

Fern reaches out and touches a nearby mycelium thread. It pulses AMBER-GOLD at her touch, and the ripple races outward like a stone dropped in water.

SFX: A gentle rustling from deeper in the chamber. Something is approaching. The threads in that direction glow slightly brighter with WARM WHITE light, illuminating a path.

VFX: The warm white glow intensifies and then, stepping out from behind a massive root column --

FUN-GUS appears.

He is a three-foot-tall AMANITA MUSHROOM -- red-orange cap with white spots, cream-colored body, a warm and open face with large expressive eyes. He wears a blue sash, and his gills glow softly with warm white light. He moves with a gentle, bouncing walk.

FUN-GUS
(spreading his arms wide)
Well, well, WELL! Visitors! Oh, this is WONDERFUL!

(bowing, cap tilting forward)
Welcome, welcome to Sillium! I'm Fun-Gus, your friendly neighborhood network guide!

BIRCH
(no fear at all)
You're a MUSHROOM. A TALKING mushroom.

FUN-GUS
(chuckling)
Well, technically, THIS --
(gestures to himself)
-- is only a small part of me. I'm like an iceberg! What you see is just the tip. Most of me is down here.

He gestures to the vast web of mycelium stretching in every direction.

FUN-GUS (CONT'D)
All of THAT is me too. And my family. And my neighbors. All connected.

FERN
(eyes wide)
The mushrooms on the surface -- they're just... the top part?

FUN-GUS
YES! Imagine if you could only see the chimneys of houses. You'd never know about the whole building underneath!

FERN
(to Birch, awed)
The mushrooms are like... periscopes. And this whole network is the submarine.

FUN-GUS
(this is his favorite topic)
See those pulses? Those lights moving through the threads? Those are messages. Trees talking to trees. Fungi talking to fungi. Everybody sharing information, sharing food, sharing warnings.

(beat, then with a conspiratorial grin)

Think of it like... the world's oldest group chat.

BIRCH
TREES can TALK?!

FUN-GUS
Not with words, exactly. With chemistry! A tree gets munched on by a caterpillar, and it sends a signal through my network -- all the way to the other trees, saying "Heads up! Bug attack! Make your leaves taste bad!"

VFX: A RED-ORANGE warning pulse races along a thread, branching to multiple endpoints. At each endpoint, a faint tree outline illuminates.

FUN-GUS (CONT'D)
An alarm system, a postal service, a restaurant delivery app, and a library, all rolled into one. Running for four hundred and fifty MILLION years.

BIRCH
That's before DINOSAURS!

FUN-GUS
WAY before dinosaurs.

Fun-Gus's expression shifts. He tilts his head, listening. His glow dims slightly.

FUN-GUS (CONT'D)
(concerned, quieter)
But right now... something's not right.

He walks toward one particular thread. The pulses along it are STUTTERING. Weak. Thinning.

FUN-GUS (CONT'D)
There's a seedling -- a baby tree -- somewhere deeper in the network. Its connection is fading. It can't get the nutrients it needs. If we don't find it and strengthen the link...

FERN
(quiet)
It'll die?

FUN-GUS
(gently)
It'll be alone. And down here, alone is the same thing.

(beat, then brightening -- turning to face both kids)

But I know every part of this network. I know exactly who can help. Want to come with me? Let me show you around.

BIRCH
(immediately)
YES.

FERN
(closing her journal, tucking it away, resolute)
Let's go.

Fun-Gus bounces ahead, and the path illuminates before him.

### ACT TWO: THE TOUR

**[5:00 - 16:00]**

---

#### ENCOUNTER 1: SPORA

**[5:00 - 6:15]**

INT. SILLIUM -- MAIN TUNNEL -- CONTINUOUS

Fun-Gus leads the kids through a wider tunnel of mycelium. The walls pulse with threading light.

SFX: A tiny, uncertain sound from behind a root nearby. A squeak, almost. Something small and shy.

Fun-Gus hears it. His expression softens immediately.

FUN-GUS
(lowering his voice, turning toward the sound)
Ah. Speaking of newcomers...

He crouches down and gently calls toward a cluster of root fibers.

FUN-GUS (CONT'D)
(softly)
It's all right. You can come out. We have guests.

Slowly, nervously, SPORA emerges.

She is TINY -- even at their already-shrunken scale, she is small. A round, pale pink-white body with enormous amber eyes. Delicate neural filaments extend from the top of her head, but they flicker and sputter -- unstable, like a lightbulb with a loose connection. She floats very slightly off the ground, bobbing.

She peeks at the kids from behind Fun-Gus's leg.

SPORA
(tiny, uncertain voice)
H-hello?

BIRCH
(melting, crouching down)
Oh my gosh. Hi!

FERN
(also crouching, speaking gently)
Hey there. What's your name?

SPORA
(stepping out slightly, still half-hidden)
I'm... Spora. I'm new.

FUN-GUS
(placing a gentle hand on Spora's head)
Spora here is one of our newest spores. She's still learning how the network works. Just like you two!

SPORA
(looking up at Fun-Gus, then at the kids)
Am I... am I doing it right?

Her filaments flicker uncertainly. One sputters out entirely. She looks embarrassed.

FERN
(warmly)
I don't think there IS a wrong way. You're here. That's the first step.

Spora looks at Fern. Her filaments flicker slightly brighter.

SPORA
(small smile)
Really?

BIRCH
Can she come with us? On the tour?

FUN-GUS
(beaming)
I was hoping you'd ask.

Spora floats up to eye level with the kids, still bobbing uncertainly but no longer hiding. She reaches out a tiny tendril and touches a passing thread. Her filaments glow AMBER-GOLD briefly, then fade.

SPORA
(trying)
I want to send a message, but I can't make it go all the way...

FERN
(watching closely)
Your light is really pretty when you try, though.

SPORA
(small, pleased sound)
...you think so?

FUN-GUS
(walking ahead)
Come on, everyone. The seedling's this way. And there are some friends I'd like you to meet along the road.

---

#### ENCOUNTER 2: LUMOS

**[6:15 - 7:30]**

INT. SILLIUM -- DARK PASSAGE -- CONTINUOUS

The tunnel narrows. The ambient glow of the mycelium threads DIMS. The warm pulsing light thins until only scattered threads remain, many of them dark. Dormant.

SFX: The network hum fades to near silence. Dripping water. A distant, echoing creak.

BIRCH
(slowing down, peering ahead)
Uh... is it supposed to get this dark?

FUN-GUS
(casually)
This section's been dormant for a while. The threads went to sleep. Happens when the trees above lose their leaves in winter. They wake up again, but sometimes they need a little help.

FERN
(gripping Birch's sleeve)
What kind of help?

A trembling glow appears ahead. Faint. Blue-green. Moving.

The glow gets brighter -- and BRIGHTER -- and then LUMOS steps around a corner.

He is a slender, delicate mushroom with a translucent bell-shaped cap that radiates ethereal blue-green bioluminescent light. His body has a translucent quality -- you can see faint network threads glowing inside him like fiber optics. He has large round pale green eyes and a slight nervous hunch.

As Lumos approaches, his light spills across the dormant threads around him. One by one, they FLICKER back to life -- dim at first, then steady cyan. He's re-illuminating the passage just by walking through it.

LUMOS
(noticing the group, startled -- his glow SURGES bright white-blue)
GAH! Oh -- oh, it's you, Fun-Gus. And -- visitors?

FUN-GUS
Lumos! Perfect timing. Everyone, this is Lumos, our lamplighter. He scouts the dark places and brings the threads back online.

LUMOS
(glow settling back to blue-green, embarrassed)
I -- yeah. Someone has to go first, right?

BIRCH
(staring, delighted)
Dude, you get BRIGHTER when you're scared!

Lumos's glow surges again.

LUMOS
(mortified)
I KNOW. It's so embarrassing. I'm literally impossible to sneak with.

Fern laughs. Even Spora giggles -- the first real sound of amusement she's made.

LUMOS (CONT'D)
(straightening up, trying for dignity)
Anyway. The path ahead was dark, but I've been relighting it. Should be clear all the way to the junction.

He turns and walks ahead, his glow spilling warm blue-green light across the tunnel. The dormant threads light up in his wake like a trail of stars.

FERN
(to Fun-Gus, quiet wonder)
He wakes them up. Just by being there.

FUN-GUS
(nodding)
Some people carry the light. Even when they don't realize it.

Lumos overhears this but says nothing. His glow steadies.

The group follows him through the newly illuminated passage, Spora floating close behind Lumos, watching his light with fascination.

---

#### ENCOUNTER 3: PUFF

**[7:30 - 8:45]**

INT. SILLIUM -- WIDE JUNCTION -- CONTINUOUS

The tunnel opens into a broad junction where several mycelium pathways converge. The threads here are active and bright -- a busy crossroads.

FUN-GUS
Now, when we reach the next section, you might want to --

SFX: A distant, building rumble. Like a sneeze gathering force.

FUN-GUS (CONT'D)
(sighing fondly)
-- cover your eyes.

From the far tunnel -- PUFF arrives.

He doesn't walk in. He BOUNCES in. A large, perfectly round, white-cream puffball -- like a giant marshmallow with a face. Enormous bright blue eyes, perpetually wide. Stubby tiny arms and legs that seem too small for his body. He is vibrating with excitement.

PUFF
(at maximum volume)
FUN-GUS! FUN-GUS I HEARD THERE WERE VISITORS AND I --

He sees the kids.

His eyes somehow get WIDER.

Cracks appear on his surface.

PUFF (CONT'D)
-- oh my gosh they're SO SMALL and SO CUTE and --

He EXPLODES.

VFX: A massive billowing cloud of golden-amber spore-dust erupts from Puff in every direction. It fills the junction like glitter in a snow globe. Everything and everyone is coated.

SFX: A comedic POOMF followed by the soft patter of spore-dust settling.

BIRCH
(coughing, laughing, covered in gold dust)
WHAT WAS THAT?!

FERN
(wiping her eyes, also laughing)
Is -- is he okay?!

Puff reforms from the cloud, slightly smaller and extremely sheepish.

PUFF
(grinning apologetically)
Sorry! Sorry! I just -- I got excited!

FUN-GUS
(brushing spore-dust off his cap, not at all surprised)
Everyone, this is Puff. He's our emergency broadcast system. When a message needs to reach the ENTIRE network at once, Puff's spore clouds carry the signal further and faster than any thread.

PUFF
(puffing up proudly -- then catching himself)
Wait, hold on, I can be calm. I can TOTALLY be calm. Watch. Watch this.

He takes a deep breath. Holds very still. His surface smooths out.

Two seconds pass.

A beetle wanders by.

Puff's eyes track the beetle.

PUFF (CONT'D)
(whispering)
Is that a BEETLE? I love BEETLES. They're so --

He BURSTS again. Another golden cloud, smaller this time but just as enthusiastic.

The kids are in hysterics. Lumos's glow flickers with laughter.

And Spora -- floating in the gently settling spore-dust -- GIGGLES. A real, full giggle. Her filaments flicker bright.

PUFF
(emerging from his own cloud, dismayed)
Okay. So calm isn't really my thing.

FUN-GUS
(arm around Puff)
Your thing is better, Puff. Come on -- you can come along. Just try to keep the bursting to a minimum.

PUFF
(bouncing after them)
NO PROMISES!

#### ENCOUNTER 4: CHANTY

**[8:45 - 10:00]**

INT. SILLIUM -- THE AMPHITHEATER -- CONTINUOUS

The tunnel opens into a natural amphitheater -- a curved chamber where the walls arc upward like the inside of a shell. Mycelium threads are woven densely here, creating a warm, acoustically rich space. The light is golden-amber.

In the center, a small audience of varied fungi sit in a loose semicircle. Some are small and round, others tall and thin. They're all leaning forward, rapt.

At the focal point stands CHANTY.

She is a regal, medium-sized mushroom with a flowing, trumpet-shaped golden-amber cap with elegant wavy ruffled edges that resemble a performer's cape. Rich apricot-gold coloring. Warm amber eyes sparkling with intelligence. She speaks with her entire body -- arms gesturing, cap swirling, voice rising and falling like music.

VFX: As she speaks, tiny golden particles drift from her cap edges -- STORY-DUST -- and in the air, the dust forms shapes. Ghostly images. Living illustrations.

CHANTY
(mid-story, theatrical)
-- and the old oak held fast! Its roots dug deep, deeper than the storm could reach, and it sent one final message through the network: "I am still here. I am still here."

The story-dust forms a ghostly oak tree, roots blazing with light, weathering a wind that shakes its branches.

The small audience SIGHS. One of them wipes an eye.

Fun-Gus clears his throat gently. Chanty looks up, spots the group, and her eyes LIGHT UP.

CHANTY
(opening her arms wide)
Oh! OH! New ears! New hearts! NOTHING is better than a fresh audience!

She sweeps over to them, cap ruffles flowing.

CHANTY (CONT'D)
(appraising the kids with delighted intensity)
Surface children! Oh, this IS a story. This is a BEGINNING.

FUN-GUS
Chanty, we're on our way to a seedling that's losing its connection. Do you know anything about --

CHANTY
(suddenly serious, the theatrics dropping)
That seedling.

She turns to the group. The story-dust shifts. A new image forms in the golden particles: a tall, magnificent tree. Then -- time passing. The tree falling. A single seed drifting to earth.

CHANTY (CONT'D)
(quieter, the performer becoming the historian)
Its mother tree once stood tall in this part of the forest. A hundred years she grew. She was a hub -- dozens of threads connected through her roots. When she fell in the great windstorm three seasons ago...

VFX: The ghostly tree topples. The golden threads connected to it go dark, one by one.

CHANTY (CONT'D)
...she left behind one seed. One chance. That seedling is the last of its line.

Silence. The stakes just deepened.

FERN
(quiet)
The last one?

CHANTY
(looking at Fern with sudden warmth)
Stories don't end when the storyteller falls, dear. They end when no one is left to carry them forward.

She looks at the fading seedling image, then back at the group.

CHANTY (CONT'D)
(clapping her hands, the performer returning)
Well? What are you standing around for? That seedling needs more than a story. It needs a CONNECTION.

FUN-GUS
(to the kids)
You heard the bard. Let's keep moving.

As they leave, Chanty falls in behind them. She doesn't ask permission. Her story isn't finished yet.

#### ENCOUNTER 5: RHIZA

**[10:00 - 11:15]**

INT. SILLIUM -- ROOT-THREAD JUNCTION -- CONTINUOUS

The tunnel descends into a massive junction where enormous tree roots descend from above and mycelium threads rise from below. This is where two worlds meet. The light here is different -- warm brown from above, cool cyan from below, and where they mix: GOLDEN.

Standing at the exact intersection -- half-emerged from the ground, never fully detached from either world -- is RHIZA.

She is ethereal, flowing. Her upper body has delicate white fungal hyphal threads; her lower body transitions seamlessly into warm brown root-like tendrils. She is semi-transparent, and tiny glowing nutrient particles flow through her body in both directions -- like golden fireflies traveling internal highways.

Her eyes are deep warm amber. Serene. She looks through you.

RHIZA
(calm, unsurprised)
Fun-Gus. You're bringing quite the procession today.

FUN-GUS
Rhiza! Everyone, this is Rhiza. She's the most important person you'll never see.

RHIZA
(gentle, amused)
I'm the handshake. Where roots meet mycelium, where trees meet fungi -- that's me. I translate.

BIRCH
Translate what?

RHIZA
Watch.

VFX: A pulse arrives through a massive root above -- WARM BROWN, slow, heavy. It reaches Rhiza. She absorbs it. Her body glows. Then a new pulse leaves her from the fungal side -- CYAN, faster, lighter. A response comes back almost immediately -- fast cyan. She absorbs it, converts it, and sends a warm brown pulse back up into the roots.

The entire exchange takes five seconds. Through Rhiza's translucent body, the kids can see the nutrient particles changing direction, changing color, flowing in real-time.

FERN
(staring, mouth open)
She's the WiFi router.

FUN-GUS
(pointing at Fern, overjoyed)
I've been saying that for CENTURIES! Nobody listens!

RHIZA
(to the kids, with quiet intensity)

### Pacing Notes for Storyboard Artists

- **Cold open (0:00-0:45):** FAST. Underground pulse, smash cut to surface, boots past mushroom. Add the stuttering thread early -- plant the seed of the problem.
- **Act One (0:50-5:00):** Build naturally. Character establishment through organic dialogue. Compress the fairy ring discovery and shrinking -- the audience knows what the show is about by now. Fun-Gus's entrance breaks reverence with warmth. End the act on URGENCY -- the seedling problem drives everything that follows.
- **Act Two (5:00-16:00):** The heart. Each encounter is a miniature story with its own arc, comedy beat, emotional truth, and scientific concept. The rhythm alternates: quiet/tender, funny/loud, emotional/deep, funny/precise, tense/cool. Each character encounter should feel like a new world opening.
- **Act Three (16:00-20:30):** Rising action. Rapid-fire character positioning -- 5-10 seconds each, visually specific. Spora's moment is the emotional peak. Truff-Low is the awe peak. Two different climaxes.
- **Act Four (20:30-21:30):** Denouement. Quick, warm goodbyes. The Ms. Hawthorn scene is a quiet mystery. Fern touching the tree is the emotional landing. Don't rush it.

### Sound Design Direction

- **Above Ground:** Natural, warm, familiar. Birdsong, wind, footsteps on leaves.
- **The Transition:** Natural sounds TRANSFORM. Birdsong becomes the hum. Wind becomes the pulse.
- **Underground -- General:** Organic ambiance. The network hums like a living thing. Different thread colors have subtly different pitches.
- **Character Themes:**
  - *Lumos:* Crystalline chimes that swell when he brightens
  - *Puff:* Comic brass/percussion POOMF with settling shimmer
  - *Chanty:* Warm strings and woodwinds, theatrical swells
  - *Rhiza:* Layered harmonics -- tree sounds (deep) and fungal sounds (high) blending
  - *Inky:* Quiet calligraphic scratching, contemplative single-note piano
  - *Stench:* Buzzing insect chorus that becomes musical -- unexpectedly beautiful
  - *Birdie:* Precision click-THWIP, satisfying mechanical sounds, maternal warmth
  - *Snapper:* Low tension strings, then crisp decisive sounds when she acts
- **Mother Oak:** Felt as much as heard. Low frequency. Resonant. Like standing inside a cello.
- **Truff-Low:** Vibrates the space. Not loud -- deep.
- **The Climax:** All character themes LAYERING together, building, converging on one moment.
