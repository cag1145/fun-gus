| Signal | Data |

| **Fantastic Fungi** (documentary) | 100% Rotten Tomatoes, ~$1M theatrical, expanded to Netflix/Amazon. Now a consumer products brand (supplements, merchandise) |

| **Paul Stamets** | 10M+ views across podcast appearances (Joe Rogan, etc.) |
| **"Wood Wide Web"** | Term has entered mainstream vocabulary via journalism and pop science |
| **Academic interest** | Clark University mycology conference (2024), University of Chicago published "Mycoaesthetics" in Critical Inquiry |
| **Radical Mycology movement** | Grassroots community combining mushrooms with environmental healing |

**The gap:** Mycology has crossed from niche science into mainstream culture via food, wellness, documentary film, and bestselling books. But there is **NO major children's IP in this space.** Fun-Gus fills a white-space gap with zero established competition.

## 7. KEY PITCH NARRATIVES

### For Investors/Partners — The Data Says:

1. **White space in a massive market.** The $7.24B kids' animation market and $60B STEM education market have zero mycology/fungi IP properties. Fun-Gus is first-mover with no competition.

2. **Proven pipeline.** Ada Twist: picture book to 4-season Netflix show in 5 years (6M+ books sold). Octonauts: picture book to $195M Sony acquisition in 13 years. The path is well-worn and repeatable.

3. **Cultural tailwind.** Mycology has gone mainstream — $31B mushroom market, 1M+ copies of Entangled Life, Fantastic Fungi became a consumer brand. But nobody has brought this to kids yet.

4. **Buyers are actively looking.** Netflix acquired StoryBots for "the next Sesame Street." Scholastic spent $186M to vertically integrate. Sony spent $195M on creature-based kids' IP. The money is in the market.

5. **Revenue ceiling is enormous.** PAW Patrol: $2B/year. Bluey: billion-dollar brand. Even modest success (Wild Kratts at $30M) validates a sustainable business. The upside is uncapped.

6. **Transmedia from day one.** The most successful kids' IPs span books, shows, toys, games, apps, and experiences. Fun-Gus was designed as transmedia from the start — 34 characters, 22 episodes, visual assets, pilot script, world bible, all ready.
