## SLIDE 3: THE INSIGHT

### The Forest Has WiFi. For Real.

Beneath every forest on Earth is a living communication network. Fungi and trees are connected by miles of underground threads called **mycelium**. Through these threads, they:

- Share food, water, and minerals
- Send chemical warnings about drought and disease
- Nurture young seedlings through "mother trees"
- Store memory across generations

Scientists call it the **"Wood Wide Web."** It's been running for **450 million years** — long before humans invented the internet.

**This is not fantasy. This is real science that sounds like fantasy.**

That's the magic of Fun-Gus.

## SLIDE 4: THE CONCEPT

### Shrink Down. Log On. Explore.

Two curious kids — a boy and a girl — and their teacher discover a glowing **fairy ring** of mushrooms. They step inside, **shrink to spore-size**, and enter **SILLIUM** — the living fungal network beneath the forest.

Underground, they meet a world of characters across **three biological kingdoms:**

- **Fungi** — heroes and villains, from friendly mentors to parasitic puppet-masters
- **Bacteria** — unlikely allies and insidious corruptors
- **Insects** — above-ground protectors, philosophers, and farmers

Every character teaches real science. Every conflict mirrors real ecology. Every story makes kids want to go outside and look at the world differently.

## SLIDE 8: THE WORLD — SILLIUM

### A Living Underground Network

**SILLIUM** is the name for the mycelium communication system in the Fun-Gus universe. It functions as both setting and plot engine:

**How It Works:**
- Glowing cyan/teal threads = data highways carrying messages
- Mushroom nodes = routing stations
- Color shifts = emotion, urgency, learning state
- Pulses of light = messages traveling in real-time

**Key Locations:**
| Location | Function | Feel |
|----------|----------|------|
| The Fairy Ring | Entry point from surface | Magical threshold |
| The Hub | Central network junction | Busy, cathedral-like |
| The Nursery | Where young spores grow | Warm, nurturing |
| The Deep | Truff-Low's memory vault | Ancient silence |
| The Edges | Network frontier | Mysterious, unexplored |
| The Recycling Zone | Dead matter → new life | Celebratory transformation |

**The world IS the lesson:** The underground network is the internet analogy that makes complex science instantly relatable.

## SLIDE 9: THE HIDDEN LAYER — FOLKLORE

### For the Adults in the Room

The teacher — **Ms. Hawthorn** — is secretly one of the *aos si* (Celtic faerie folk). Not Tinkerbell. Not Tolkien. The old kind. Human-appearing, ancient, deeply connected to natural systems.

**She's been bringing children to SILLIUM for generations.**

This is NEVER confirmed in the show. Only hinted:
- She hums melodies nobody recognizes
- Animals respond to her presence
- She's never lost in the forest
- Old neighbors remember her "from when they were children"
- The fairy ring glows brighter when she's near

**Kids see:** A wonderful teacher who knows a lot about nature.
**Adults see:** Something much deeper.

**Why this matters commercially:** Parents rewatch. Parents discuss. Parents share. The folklore layer creates adult engagement that drives recommendations and social media buzz — without adding anything inappropriate for children.
