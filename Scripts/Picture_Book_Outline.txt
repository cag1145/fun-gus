PICTURE BOOK: MY FUNGAL ADVENTURE
===================================
Format: 32 pages, large illustrations, minimal text
Audience: Ages 4-6 (read-aloud), 6-8 (independent)
Status: Manuscript Complete

SPECIFICATIONS
--------------
- Trim size: 10" x 8.5" landscape (KDP: 10" x 8")
- Page count: 32 pages (including front/back matter)
- Text per spread: 1-3 sentences (~35-65 words)
- Illustration style: Pixar-quality 3D render, soft cinematic lighting
- Total word count: ~636 words
- Read-aloud timing: ~3.5 minutes

STRUCTURE
---------
Setup: 2 spreads (compressed — walk, discovery, fairy ring, shrink, arrival)
Character tour: 10 spreads (each introduces one character and their "job")
Climax + Resolution: 2 spreads

Narrative throughline: A seedling is losing its connection to the network.
Fun-Gus leads the kids on a journey to find help. Each character they meet
has a unique ability. At the climax, EVERY character contributes to save the
seedling — proving the lesson: the network needs them all.

PAGE-BY-PAGE OUTLINE
--------------------

FRONT MATTER
- Page 1: Half-title (mycelium lettering, cyan pulse)
- Pages 2-3: Title spread (forest establishing shot, three figures on trail)

THE STORY

Pages 4-5: INTO THE RING
- Birch spots the fairy ring, grabs Fern's hand, they step inside
- Text: Discovery + entry combined into single moment
- Visual: Forest clearing, glowing fairy ring, Ms. Hawthorn watching from trail
- Ms. Hawthorn does NOT enter the ring (faerie hint: she doesn't need to)

Pages 6-7: DOWN TO SILLIUM ★ HERO SPREAD
- Kids shrink, fall through golden light, land in Sillium cavern
- Text: "The world grew BIGGER." Fun-Gus greets them.
- Visual: Dynamic shrinking + cavern reveal, Fun-Gus with open arms

Pages 8-9: THE GUIDE — Fun-Gus
- Fun-Gus explains the network: every thread is a conversation
- Introduces the problem: a seedling is losing its connection
- Text: "But today we have a problem..."
- Science: Mycorrhizal networks, the "Wood Wide Web"

Pages 10-11: THE LITTLE LEARNER — Spora
- Tiny, shy spore emerges. Flickering glow. Never sent a message before.
- Text: "I'm new. I don't know how to do anything yet."
- Fun-Gus: "That's where everyone starts."
- Science: Spores — how fungi reproduce

Pages 12-13: THE LAMPLIGHTER — Lumos
- Path goes dark. Lumos lights the way — brighter when scared.
- Text: "Being brave doesn't mean you're not afraid."
- Visual: Glowing translucent bell-cap, re-illuminating dormant threads
- Science: Bioluminescence in fungi (Mycena chlorophos)

Pages 14-15: THE MESSENGER — Puff
- Round puffball BURSTS in golden spores. Comedy beat.
- Text: "Sorry! I just get so EXCITED!"
- Visual: Everyone coated in golden spore-dust, laughing
- Science: Puffball spore dispersal (7 trillion spores per puffball)

Pages 16-17: THE STORYTELLER — Chanty
- Golden chanterelle tells the seedling's history through story-dust
- Text: "Its mother tree once stood the tallest in this grove."
- Visual: Story-dust coalesces into ghostly image of great oak
- Science: Mother trees, family groups in forests

Pages 18-19: THE TRANSLATOR — Rhiza
- Half-mushroom, half-root. Glows where two worlds meet.
- Text: "Trees give sugar. Fungi give water. I make sure they understand each other."
- Visual: Nutrient particles flowing both directions through her body
- Science: Mycorrhizal symbiosis — real nutrient exchange

Pages 20-21: THE SCRIBE — Inky
- Elegant ink cap writing on cavern walls, cap dissolving into ink
- Text: "Everything worth keeping costs something."
- Visual: White-to-purple-black gradient, calligraphic marks on walls
- Science: Ink cap autodigestion (deliquescence)

Pages 22-23: THE MISUNDERSTOOD ONE — Stench
- Birch wrinkles his nose. But Stench is surrounded by insect friends.
- Text: "My smell brings helpers. Without them, spores don't travel."
- Fern writes: "Important things aren't always pretty."
- Science: Stinkhorn insect attraction for spore dispersal

Pages 24-25: THE LAUNCHER — Birdie
- Cup-shaped bird's nest fungus with spectacles and clipboard. THWIP!
- Text: "Right on time. Every delivery matters."
- Visual: Spore-packet arcing across cavern in perfect trajectory
- Science: Bird's nest fungi raindrop-launched spore packets

Pages 26-27: THE PROTECTOR — Snapper
- Something dark slithers. Snapper catches it calmly.
- Text: "Not everything in the forest plays fair. That's why I'm here."
- Visual: Cool blue-gray armor-like caps, web-like trap threads
- Science: Oyster mushrooms are predatory — they catch nematodes

Pages 28-29: ALL TOGETHER ★ HERO SPREAD
- They reach the seedling. Every character uses their unique ability.
- Spora touches the thread. Her glow STABILIZES. Golden pulse radiates.
- The seedling blazes with light. TRIUMPH.
- Visual: Radial composition — Spora at center, light expanding outward
- Science: Trees receive nutrients through fungal networks

Pages 30-31: GOING HOME
- Back on the surface. Forest looks the same but they know its secret.
- Birch touches oak bark. Fern touches soil. Network glows beneath.
- Text: "The forest was talking this whole time. We just couldn't hear it."
- Ms. Hawthorn smiles. She already knew. (Faerie hint)
- Visual: Cross-section shows network + tiny character silhouettes below

BACK MATTER
- Page 32: "The Real Science!" — character-by-character real species info + mycorrhizae facts
