INFOGRAPHIC: NATURE'S INTERNET VS. HUMAN INTERNET
===================================================
Format: Single illustrated page (printable poster)
Dimensions: 18x24 inches (poster) or 8.5x11 (handout)
Status: Concept Phase

TITLE
-----
"NATURE'S INTERNET vs. HUMAN INTERNET"
Subtitle: "Who did it first?"

TAGLINE
-------
"Nature was online first."

VISUAL CONCEPT
--------------
Split design: Left side = fungal network (organic, glowing)
              Right side = human internet (geometric, digital)
              Center = character bridge (Fun-Gus explaining)

COMPARISON TABLE
----------------

| FUNGAL NETWORK          | HUMAN INTERNET           |
|-------------------------|--------------------------|
| Mycelium threads        | Fiber optic cables       |
| Tree root connections   | Computers & servers      |
| Chemical signals        | Data packets             |
| Nutrient sharing        | File sharing             |
| Warning messages        | Alert notifications      |
| Mother trees (hubs)     | Data centers             |
| Spores                  | Wireless signals         |
| 450+ million years old  | ~50 years old            |

KEY STATISTICS
--------------
(visual callout boxes)

- "One teaspoon of forest soil contains MILES of mycelium"
- "A single fungal network can connect HUNDREDS of trees"
- "Trees share up to 40% of their food through the network"
- "Scientists call it the 'Wood Wide Web'"

FUN-GUS SAYS
------------
(character speech bubble)
"We've been connecting the forest since before dinosaurs existed.
You just discovered electricity!"

BOTTOM SECTION
--------------
"What they have in common:"
- Both send messages
- Both share resources
- Both have hubs and connections
- Both are stronger because they're connected
