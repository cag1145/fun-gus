## THE CONCEPT

**Logline:** A kid-friendly mycology sci-fi universe where fungi humanize Earth's original intelligent communication network, teaching cooperation, communication, and resilience.

**The Hook:** "The Forest Has WiFi" — Nature invented the internet 450 million years before humans.

**Tagline:** "Nature was online first."

---

## THE STORY

Two curious kids — a boy and a girl — on a nature walk with their teacher discover a glowing fairy ring. They step inside, shrink to spore-size, and enter **SILLIUM** — the living fungal network beneath the forest. Guided by **Fun-Gus**, they discover how trees talk, how fungi share, and how cooperation built Earth's most resilient systems.

---

## THE SECRET LAYER

The teacher is secretly a **faerie** (Celtic/European folklore tradition — no wings, no sparkles, fully human-appearing). She's been tending human connection to nature for centuries. Fun-Gus is her old friend.

**Kids see:** A nice, magical-feeling teacher
**Adults wonder:** "Wait... is she...?"
**Rule:** Never confirmed. Only hinted.

## CONTENT RULES

- **Always kid-friendly** — No exceptions
- **No obscenity** — Ever
- **No villains** — Only natural challenges (drought, blight, disturbance)
- **Two levels** — Kids get adventure, adults get depth
- **Science accurate** — Real mycology, made accessible
- **Tongue-in-cheek OK** — Adults can catch references kids won't
