Script Excerpt

Cold Open
[FADE IN: A forest trail. Late afternoon. Golden light through the canopy. Three figures walk the path.] [BIRCH runs ahead, magnifying glass raised. FERN walks carefully, writing in her nature journal. MS. HAWTHORN follows, smiling. A robin sits on her shoulder.] BIRCH Ms. Hawthorn! What's that mushroom? The red one with the spots! MS. HAWTHORN (warmly) That's an amanita, Birch. Beautiful, isn't it? Fungi are some of the oldest living things on Earth. FERN (not looking up from journal) How old? MS. HAWTHORN Older than trees. Older than flowers. They were here before almost everything else. [BEAT. FERN looks up. Impressed.] FERN That's... really old. MS. HAWTHORN (glancing toward a clearing, smiling) And they've been busy the whole time.
The Fairy Ring
[BIRCH pushes through a fern frond and STOPS. His magnifying glass drops to his chest.] BIRCH (whispered) Fern. Come look at this. [A PERFECT CIRCLE OF MUSHROOMS in a forest clearing. Each cap glows faintly at its edges — warm white light, pulsing like breathing. The grass inside is impossibly green.] FERN A fairy ring. They're real. [They look back at MS. HAWTHORN. She stands at the edge of the clearing, smiling. She does NOT step closer.] MS. HAWTHORN Go ahead. Some things you have to discover for yourselves. [BIRCH and FERN step inside. The glow INTENSIFIES. A deep, resonant TONE — like a singing bowl. Golden light envelops them.]
