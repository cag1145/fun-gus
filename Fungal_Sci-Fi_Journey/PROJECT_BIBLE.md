## 1. PROJECT OVERVIEW

### Logline
A transmedia children's universe that humanizes fungal networks through memorable characters, teaching communication, cooperation, and resilience in natural systems.

### Elevator Pitch
*Fun-Gus* is a kid-friendly mycology sci-fi universe where fungi and trees form Earth's original intelligent communication network called SILLIUM. Through picture books, digital stories, games, and more, children discover that nature invented the internet millions of years before humans—and that cooperation, not competition, built our world's most resilient systems.

### Core Themes
- Nature as technology
- Cooperation and communication
- Learning and growth
- Resilience through connection
- Science as wonder (not chore)

### Comparable Properties
- *Magic School Bus* (educational adventure)
- *Pikmin* (tiny world, cooperation)
- *Octonauts* (creature-focused education)
- *Story Bots* (science through character)

## 2. CORE CONCEPT

### The Big Idea
Fungi are Earth's first internet. Long before wires or screens, living networks beneath the soil allowed trees and fungi to share information, nutrients, and warnings. **Technology did not invent intelligence. It discovered it.**

### The Hook: "The Forest Has WiFi"
Children learn that beneath every forest is a living network—and through our characters, they can see it working.

### The Entry Point
Two curious girls on a nature walk with their female teacher discover a glowing mushroom ring. When they step inside, they shrink to spore-size and enter the SILLIUM network, where they meet Fun-Gus and friends.

### The Shrinking Mechanism
- **Method:** Stepping into a fairy ring (glowing mushroom circle)
- **Visual:** Sparkle/glow effect, world grows enormous around them
- **Return:** Fun-Gus guides them back to the ring to return to normal size
- **Rule:** Time moves differently underground (minutes = hours above? TBD)

## 3. CHARACTERS

### Primary Fungal Characters

#### FUN-GUS (The Guide)
- **Role:** Teacher, guide, warm mentor
- **Personality:** Curious, joyful, patient, never condescending
- **Visual:** Red-orange amanita-style cap with white spots, cream body, blue sash with golden Sillium interface device, warm glowing gills
- **Speech Style:** Enthusiastic but clear, uses analogies kids understand
- **Catchphrase:** TBD
- **Teaches:** How networks work, cooperation, patience

#### SPORA (The Learner)
- **Role:** Newborn spore, audience surrogate for "not knowing"
- **Personality:** Curious, eager, makes mistakes, asks "why?"
- **Visual:** Tiny round pale pink-white body, large amber eyes, glowing neural filaments on head (unstable/flickering)
- **Arc:** From floating observer to stable network participant
- **Teaches:** It's okay not to know, learning is a journey

#### TRUFF-LOW (The Elder)
- **Role:** Ancient memory keeper, underground data vault
- **Personality:** Slow, quiet, wise, speaks rarely but meaningfully
- **Visual:** Large dark purple-gray truffle, moss-covered, partially buried, orange glowing cracks that activate when sharing knowledge
- **Rule:** When Truff-Low speaks, it matters
- **Teaches:** History, patience, deep time, memory

### Human Characters

#### THE GIRLS (Names TBD)
- **Number:** Two girls (best friends)
- **Relationship:** Friends, not sisters - met through shared curiosity about nature
- **Age:** 7-8 years old
- **Personality:** Curious, notice small things others miss, brave together
- **Visual Direction:** Rainboots, nature-ready clothes, messy hair, maybe magnifying glass or nature journal
- **Name Ideas:** Myla, Fern, Hazel, Sylvie, Rue, Luna, Ivy, Willow
- **Dynamic:** One slightly more cautious, one slightly more bold - they balance each other

#### THE TEACHER (Name TBD) — *SECRET FAERIE*
- **Surface Role:** "A fun gal" - the adult who enables the adventure
- **True Nature:** She is secretly a faerie (folklore tradition, NOT Tolkien/high fantasy)
- **Faerie Type:** Old-world, Celtic/European folklore - no wings, no pointed ears, no glitter
- **Personality:** Encouraging, asks questions instead of giving answers, strangely knowledgeable, never seems surprised by magic
- **Visual Direction:** Practical outdoor clothing, earth tones, perhaps slightly old-fashioned details, warm demeanor, eyes that seem to know more than she says
- **Name Ideas:** Ms. Fernwood, Ms. Meadows, Ms. Moss, Ms. Hawthorn, Ms. Rowan
- **The Secret:** She can access Sillium because faeries and fungi have ancient connections (fairy rings exist for a reason)
- **How It's Hinted (for adult readers):**
  - She hums songs no one recognizes
  - Animals and plants seem to respond to her
  - She's never lost in the forest
  - She knows the girls' questions before they ask
  - Old folklore references slip into her speech
  - She's been teaching "for a very long time"
  - The fairy ring glows brighter when she's near
- **Kid Experience:** Just a really nice, magical-feeling teacher
- **Adult Experience:** "Wait... is she...?"

### Supporting Fungal Characters (Expanded)

#### THE RECYCLERS (Decomposers)
- **Role:** Break down dead matter, return nutrients to soil
- **Personality:** Cheerful, busy, see beauty in decay ("Nothing is wasted!")
- **Visual:** Smaller fungi, work in groups, warm brown/orange tones
- **Teaches:** Death is part of life's cycle, recycling, transformation

#### THE LICHENS (The Partnerships)
- **Role:** Fungi + algae living as one organism
- **Personality:** Speak in "we," finish each other's sentences, deeply bonded
- **Visual:** Crusty, colorful patches on rocks/trees, two-toned glow
- **Teaches:** Symbiosis at its deepest - two becoming one

#### BRACKET (The Shelf Fungus)
- **Role:** Network relay station, lives on tree trunks
- **Personality:** Stoic, dependable, "I've seen things from up here"
- **Visual:** Shelf-like, tiered, positioned high on trees
- **Teaches:** Different perspectives, patience, longevity

### Tree Characters

#### MOTHER OAK
- **Role:** Elder tree, major network hub
- **Personality:** Nurturing, sends resources to struggling seedlings
- **Visual:** Massive root system, warm presence, deep voice
- **Teaches:** Parental care exists throughout nature

#### THE SEEDLINGS
- **Role:** Young trees learning to connect
- **Personality:** Eager, nervous, dependent on network
- **Visual:** Tiny above ground, reaching roots below
- **Teaches:** Everyone starts small

### Other Network Inhabitants

#### THE SPRINGTAILS (Tiny Arthropods)
- **Role:** Spore carriers, "mail delivery"
- **Personality:** Bouncy, energetic, easily distracted
- **Visual:** Tiny, hop everywhere, covered in spore dust
- **Teaches:** Small creatures matter, everyone has a job

#### THE EARTHWORMS (The Tunnel Makers)
- **Role:** Create pathways, aerate soil
- **Personality:** Humble, hardworking, "just doing my part"
- **Visual:** Segmented, gentle, leave trails
- **Teaches:** Unglamorous work is essential

### Antagonists & Villains

> **CREATIVE DIRECTION UPDATE (2026-02-08):** The original "no villains" philosophy has been replaced. Both fungi and bacteria can be heroes or villains. The moral axis is **connectors vs. exploiters** — those who share the network vs. those who drain it. This is more scientifically honest (parasitic fungi and beneficial bacteria both exist in nature) and creates richer storytelling. The lesson isn't "fungi good, bacteria bad" — it's "every group has those who cooperate and those who exploit. Character is a choice, not a species."

#### Design Principles for Antagonists
- Villains have **motivations**, not just malice — survival, hunger, fear, ambition
- Both fungi AND bacteria can be good or evil — no species-wide judgments
- Antagonists teach through contrast: exploitation highlights why cooperation matters
- Kid-appropriate threat level — scary enough to create stakes, not enough for nightmares
- Some antagonists can be redeemed; others are genuine ongoing threats

#### VILLAIN FUNGI

**CORDYCEPS (The Puppeteer)**
- **Role:** Parasitic fungus that hijacks other organisms
- **Personality:** Charismatic, persuasive, believes control is efficiency
- **Visual:** Elegant, unsettling beauty — bright orange-red tendrils, smooth voice
- **Philosophy:** "Why cooperate when you can command?"
- **Threat:** Can override individual will, turns network members into extensions of itself
- **Real Science:** Based on *Ophiocordyceps* fungi that control insect behavior
- **Teaches:** Manipulation vs. genuine cooperation, consent matters

**ARMILLARIA (The Strangler)**
- **Role:** Aggressive fungus that kills trees to feed itself
- **Personality:** Patient, relentless, justifies destruction as "natural order"
- **Visual:** Dark honey-colored, spreading rhizomorphs like black roots, massive underground presence
- **Philosophy:** "Everything dies eventually. I just speed it up."
- **Threat:** One of the largest organisms on Earth — can consume entire sections of forest
- **Real Science:** *Armillaria* (honey fungus) is a real tree-killing pathogen; the largest known organism is an Armillaria colony spanning 2,385 acres
- **Teaches:** Unchecked growth destroys communities, taking without giving back

**THE HOARDER (Rogue Mycelium)**
- **Role:** A network node that intercepts and keeps nutrients instead of passing them on
- **Personality:** Fearful, hoarding out of scarcity anxiety, not pure malice
- **Visual:** Bloated, tangled mass of threads, dull glow from stolen resources
- **Philosophy:** "There's never enough. I have to keep what I can."
- **Threat:** Creates dead zones in the network where resources stop flowing
- **Redeemable?** Yes — could learn that sharing creates more abundance than hoarding
- **Teaches:** Fear-driven selfishness harms everyone, including yourself

#### VILLAIN BACTERIA

**THE BLIGHT SWARM**
- **Role:** Pathogenic bacteria that attack plant roots and disrupt mycorrhizal connections
- **Personality:** Hive-mind, relentless, no individual voice — acts like a natural disaster with intent
- **Visual:** Sickly yellow-green cloud of tiny rod-shaped creatures, leaving brown decay in their wake
- **Philosophy:** None — pure consumption, force of nature
- **Threat:** Can sever tree-fungus connections, isolate network nodes, spread rapidly
- **Real Science:** Bacterial pathogens like *Agrobacterium* and *Ralstonia* attack plant roots
- **Teaches:** Some threats can't be reasoned with — they must be resisted collectively

**TOXICUS (The Corruptor)**
- **Role:** Bacterial agent that poisons network signals, turning messages into misinformation
- **Personality:** Clever, subtle, works from within — corrupts rather than destroys
- **Visual:** Translucent, hard to see, leaves a faint sickly shimmer on infected threads
- **Philosophy:** "If you can't trust your messages, you can't trust anyone."
- **Threat:** Network members receive false warnings, wrong nutrient requests — cooperation breaks down from within
- **Teaches:** Misinformation is as dangerous as direct attack, trust must be maintained

#### HERO BACTERIA (Allies)

**NITRO (The Fixer)**
- **Role:** Nitrogen-fixing bacterium, essential network ally
- **Personality:** Hardworking, humble, underappreciated — fungi sometimes distrust him despite his contributions
- **Visual:** Small, round, warm green glow, lives in root nodules
- **Arc:** Has to prove himself to a network that's been taught "bacteria = threat"
- **Real Science:** Rhizobium bacteria convert atmospheric nitrogen into usable nutrients — plants literally can't survive without them
- **Teaches:** Prejudice is wrong, allies come from unexpected places, judge by actions not identity

**THE SOIL CREW (Decomposer Bacteria)**
- **Role:** Work alongside The Recyclers (fungi) to break down dead matter
- **Personality:** Scrappy, fast-moving, work in shifts — friendly rivals with the fungal Recyclers
- **Visual:** Tiny, varied shapes (rods, spirals, dots), warm brown-orange tones
- **Dynamic:** Good-natured competition with Recyclers — "We got here first!" / "Quality over speed!"
- **Teaches:** Different approaches to the same goal, teamwork across groups

#### ENVIRONMENTAL THREATS (Non-Character)

These remain as impersonal forces that amplify villain activity or create crises:

**THE DROUGHT** — Network strains, resources scarce, cooperation tested. Hoarders get worse, heroes step up.

**THE DISTURBANCE (Human Activity)** — Construction, pollution, clearing. Creates wounds that opportunistic villains exploit. Humans aren't evil — they don't know what's below.

**THE IMBALANCE** — When villain organisms gain too much ground, the ecosystem tips. The real danger is never one bad actor — it's systemic breakdown.

#### The Core Conflict Framework
- **Not species vs. species** — connectors vs. exploiters across all species
- **Cooperation is fragile** — it must be actively maintained and defended
- **Villains exploit what heroes build** — parasites need networks to exist
- **Balance, not victory** — the goal is restoring ecosystem equilibrium, not destroying all enemies
- **Gray zones exist** — The Hoarder isn't evil, just afraid. Armillaria is doing what it evolved to do. The line between "natural process" and "villain" is sometimes blurry, and that's the point

## 4. WORLD RULES

### SILLIUM (The Network)
- A living, planet-wide fungal communication system
- **Not a character**—it's the environment/infrastructure
- Glowing threads = data paths
- Pulses moving through threads = messages
- Nodes (mushrooms) = routing intelligence
- Color shifts = urgency, emotion, or learning state

### Color Language
| Color | Meaning | Associated With |
|-------|---------|-----------------|
| Amber/Gold | Learning, curiosity, new growth | Spora |
| Cyan/Teal | Communication, active network traffic | Sillium, trees |
| Warm White | Trust, guidance, safety | Fun-Gus |
| Deep Green/Moss | Memory, storage, history | Truff-Low |
| Red/Orange | Warning, urgency | Danger signals |

### Scale Rules
- Humans shrink to approximately spore-size
- Mushrooms become building-sized
- Mycelium threads are like highways/tunnels
- Soil particles are like boulders

### What the Network Can Do
- Send chemical messages between trees
- Share nutrients (sugar, water, minerals)
- Warn of dangers (insects, disease, drought)
- Store information/memory (in organisms like Truff-Low)

## 5. THE HIDDEN LAYER: Folklore & Faerie Lore

> *"The old stories weren't wrong. They just didn't have microscopes."*

### The Folklore Connection

This universe operates on two levels:
1. **Surface (Kids):** Science-based wonder about fungal networks
2. **Hidden (Adults):** Ancient folklore was describing the same thing

The faeries of European folklore weren't fantasy—they were beings who understood and tended the living networks that humans couldn't see. Fairy rings, sacred groves, offerings at tree roots—these were real practices acknowledging real systems.

### What Faeries Are (In This Universe)

**NOT Tolkien/Disney:**
- No wings
- No pointed ears
- No sparkles or wands
- No tiny flying creatures
- No "Tinkerbell" aesthetic

**YES Folklore/Celtic:**
- Beings deeply connected to natural systems
- Appear human but subtly *other*
- Ancient, patient, operating on different timescales
- Guardians/tenders of ecological balance
- Can perceive what humans cannot (including Sillium)
- Associated with liminal spaces (forest edges, twilight, seasons changing)

### The Teacher's Secret

The teacher is one of the *aos sí* (people of the mounds) — folklore beings who live alongside humans but perceive the deeper networks of nature.

**Why She Teaches:**
- She's been watching humans lose connection to nature
- Children can still sense wonder; adults have forgotten
- She's planting seeds — kids who understand the network

**Why She Chose These Girls:**
- They notice things
- They ask questions
- They're ready to see

**Her Relationship with Fun-Gus:**
- They know each other (old friends? colleagues?)
- She's been bringing human children to Sillium for generations
- Fun-Gus trusts her completely

### Hints & Easter Eggs (For Attentive Adults)

These should be SUBTLE — never explained, never confirmed:

| Hint | Where It Appears |
|------|------------------|
| She hums melodies that sound ancient | Background detail |
| Birds land near her without fear | Illustrations |
| Her name is from tree/plant folklore | Character naming |
| She references "the old ways" casually | Dialogue |
| She's been teaching "longer than you'd think" | If asked her age |
| The fairy ring glows brighter near her | Visual detail |
| She's never surprised by anything magical | Story beats |
| Old Mrs. [Someone] "remembers her from childhood" | Background lore |
| She knows exactly where the fairy ring is | Plot detail |
| Fun-Gus greets her like an old friend | Interaction |

### Real Folklore Connections

| Folklore Element | Scientific Reality | Story Integration |
|------------------|-------------------|-------------------|
| Fairy rings | Mycelium growth patterns | Entry to Sillium |
| Sacred groves | Old-growth forest networks | Network hubs |
| Offerings at tree roots | Nutrient exchange points | Gratitude rituals |
| "Don't disturb the mushrooms" | Network damage | Respect for system |
| Faeries as nature guardians | Ecological balance | Teacher's true role |
| Changelings left in nature | Children who "return different" | Kids changed by Sillium |

### Tone Guidelines for Folklore Layer

- **Mystery, not explanation** — never confirm she's a faerie
- **Wonder, not fear** — folklore faeries can be tricky, but teacher is benevolent
- **Respect, not appropriation** — draw from real traditions thoughtfully
- **Subtle, not obvious** — kids shouldn't notice; adults should wonder

## 6. LOCATIONS & GEOGRAPHY

### The Surface World

#### THE FOREST
- **Type:** Temperate mixed forest (oaks, maples, birches)
- **Feel:** Familiar, accessible, "could be your local woods"
- **Key Locations:**
  - **The Fairy Ring:** Entry point, clearing in the forest
  - **The Old Oak:** Massive tree, visible landmark
  - **The Stream:** Water source, connects different areas
  - **The Edge:** Where forest meets human spaces

#### THE FAIRY RING
- **Appearance:** Circle of mushrooms in a clearing
- **When Active:** Glows softly, especially at dawn/dusk
- **Entry Requirement:** Step inside together (cooperation theme)
- **Secret:** Responds to the teacher's presence

### The Underground World (Sillium)

#### THE ENTRY CHAMBER
- **First Area:** Where visitors arrive after shrinking
- **Feel:** Welcoming, safe, like a foyer
- **Features:** Soft glow, Fun-Gus often waits here
- **Function:** Transition space, adjustment period

#### THE MAIN NETWORK (The Highways)
- **Appearance:** Vast tunnel systems of mycelium
- **Feel:** Like being inside a living circuit board
- **Features:** Glowing threads, pulsing messages, branching paths
- **Scale:** Endless, impossible to see it all

#### THE HUB (Under the Old Oak)
- **Role:** Major network junction, lots of traffic
- **Appearance:** Cathedral-like space, roots descending
- **Feel:** Busy, important, central
- **Who's There:** Many fungi, constant activity

#### THE NURSERY
- **Role:** Where young spores develop
- **Appearance:** Soft, warm, protected alcoves
- **Feel:** Gentle, nurturing
- **Who's There:** Spora came from here

#### THE DEEP (Truff-Low's Domain)
- **Role:** Ancient storage, memory archives
- **Appearance:** Dark, quiet, massive underground truffle
- **Feel:** Cathedral silence, deep time
- **Who's There:** Truff-Low, rarely disturbed

#### THE EDGES (Network Boundaries)
- **Role:** Where Sillium meets non-connected areas
- **Appearance:** Threads thin out, darkness beyond
- **Feel:** Frontier, mystery, "here be dragons"
- **Story Potential:** Expansion, new connections, exploration

#### THE RECYCLING ZONE
- **Role:** Where dead matter is processed
- **Appearance:** Busy, colorful, transformation visible
- **Feel:** Not morbid — celebratory! "Nothing is wasted"
- **Who's There:** The Recyclers, always working

### Location Design Principles

- **Organic Architecture:** No straight lines, everything grown
- **Scale Reinforces Wonder:** Everything massive from spore-perspective
- **Light = Life:** Darker areas are dormant/ancient, bright areas are active
- **Connected, Not Mapped:** Sillium can't be fully charted — it's alive

- **Title:** *My Fun-Gal Adventure*
- **Format:** 24-32 pages, large illustrations, minimal text
- **Audience:** Ages 4-6 (read-aloud), 6-8 (independent)

- Pixar-quality 3D render style
- Soft, cinematic lighting
- Bioluminescent glows
- Kid-friendly but not "babyish"

## 11. STORY IDEAS & EPISODES

### Core Story: First Contact
The anchor story—girls + teacher discover the network. Used for digital story and picture book.

### Episode Ideas (Comics, Future Stories)

#### Season 1: Learning the Network

1. **"The Thirsty Tree"**
   - Problem: Young tree can't reach water
   - Solution: Network redirects nutrients
   - Lesson: Cooperation

2. **"Spora's First Message"**
   - Problem: Spora wants to help but doesn't know how
   - Solution: Fun-Gus teaches her to carry a signal
   - Lesson: Everyone can contribute

3. **"The Warning"**
   - Problem: Insects attacking trees on one side of forest
   - Solution: Network sends chemical warning to prepare others
   - Lesson: Communication protects community

4. **"The Recyclers' Gift"**
   - Problem: A fallen tree seems "sad"
   - Solution: The Recyclers show how death becomes new life
   - Lesson: Endings are also beginnings

5. **"Too Many Messages"**
   - Problem: Network is overloaded, signals getting confused
   - Solution: Learn to prioritize, patience, waiting your turn
   - Lesson: Communication requires listening too

6. **"The Seedling's First Day"**
   - Problem: New seedling is scared to connect to network
   - Solution: Girls and Spora welcome them, share their own "first day"
   - Lesson: Everyone is new sometime

#### Season 2: Deeper Into Sillium

7. **"Truff-Low Wakes Up"**
   - Problem: Something is wrong but no one knows why
   - Solution: Truff-Low shares memory of similar past event
   - Lesson: History and elders have value

8. **"The Newcomer"**
   - Problem: New plant species arrives, doesn't know how to connect
   - Solution: Characters help it join the network
   - Lesson: Inclusion, welcoming others

9. **"The Break"**
   - Problem: Part of network damaged by construction above
   - Solution: Characters find alternate routes, network heals
   - Lesson: Resilience, adaptation

10. **"The Deep Dark"**
    - Problem: Message needs to reach somewhere no one's been
    - Solution: Journey to network edges, discover new connections
    - Lesson: Courage, exploration

11. **"Two Become One"**
    - Problem: A fungus and algae are arguing
    - Solution: They learn to become lichen — partnership
    - Lesson: Differences can create something new

12. **"The Long Sleep"**
    - Problem: Winter is coming, network slows down
    - Solution: Learn about dormancy, patience, seasons
    - Lesson: Rest is part of growth

#### Season 3: Connectors vs. Exploiters

17. **"The Puppeteer"**
    - Problem: A Cordyceps colony arrives at the network edge, taking control of insects and redirecting them
    - Twist: Cordyceps is charismatic — offers "order" and "efficiency" to a struggling part of the network
    - Solution: Characters must convince network members that cooperation by choice is better than control
    - Lesson: Manipulation isn't leadership, even when it looks like it's working

18. **"Nitro's Trial"**
    - Problem: A bacterial ally (Nitro) is blamed when the Blight Swarm attacks — "bacteria did this!"
    - Twist: The network's prejudice against all bacteria nearly costs them their most important ally
    - Solution: Fun-Gus and the girls vouch for Nitro; his nitrogen-fixing saves a dying section of forest
    - Lesson: Judging a whole group by its worst members is wrong and dangerous

19. **"The Hungry Dark"**
    - Problem: Armillaria is slowly killing trees on the network's west side — resources are drying up
    - Twist: Armillaria isn't evil in the traditional sense — it's doing what it evolved to do. But it's destroying the community.
    - Solution: Network must create boundaries and reroute around the damage — can't destroy Armillaria, must coexist
    - Lesson: Some threats can't be defeated, only managed. Resilience means adapting.

20. **"The Hoarder's Fear"**
    - Problem: A network node stops passing nutrients — hoarding everything out of fear of drought
    - Twist: The Hoarder isn't greedy — it experienced a drought that nearly killed it, and it's terrified
    - Solution: Spora (who understands being scared and new) reaches the Hoarder; network promises mutual support
    - Lesson: Fear makes us selfish, but trust is built through showing up for each other
    - **Redeemable villain arc**

21. **"Whispers in the Network"**
    - Problem: Messages are arriving wrong — warnings where there's no danger, silence where help is needed
    - Twist: Toxicus (corruptor bacteria) has infiltrated the signal paths, turning the network against itself
    - Solution: Characters must find the source of misinformation and restore trust in the network's signals
    - Lesson: Lies and misinformation can break a community faster than any outside attack

22. **"The Alliance"**
    - Problem: Blight Swarm + Cordyceps attack simultaneously — too much for the fungal network alone
    - Twist: The only way to win is fungi AND bacteria working together — Nitro, the Soil Crew, and the Recyclers form an unprecedented cross-kingdom alliance
    - Solution: Combined defense holds. The network is stronger for including everyone.
    - Lesson: The biggest threats require the broadest alliances — diversity is strength
    - **Season finale energy**

## 13. CONTENT GUIDELINES

> **CORE RULE: Always kid-friendly. No exceptions.**

### Language Standards

**NEVER USE:**
- Profanity or obscenity of any kind
- Crude humor (bathroom jokes, body function humor)
- Insults or mean-spirited language
- Scary or threatening language
- Words that could be misread as inappropriate

**ALWAYS USE:**
- Clear, accessible vocabulary
- Positive framing (what TO do, not what NOT to do)
- Encouraging tone
- Age-appropriate complexity

### Content Standards

**APPROPRIATE:**
- Natural life cycles (death as transformation, not scary)
- Real conflict with antagonists who have motivations (parasites, pathogens, hoarders)
- Cooperation and helping themes — made stronger by contrast with exploitation
- Heroes and villains existing in BOTH fungi and bacteria (no species-wide judgments)
- Scientific accuracy made accessible — including parasitism and competition
- Wonder and curiosity
- Mistakes as learning opportunities
- Moral gray zones (some antagonists are redeemable, some are just doing what they evolved to do)
- Prejudice as a theme (hero bacteria distrusted by fungi who assume "all bacteria are bad")

**AVOID:**
- Graphic violence (conflict is network-based: signal corruption, resource theft, severed connections — not physical combat)
- Pure evil without motivation (every antagonist has a reason)
- Species-wide judgments ("all bacteria are bad" is explicitly wrong in this universe)
- Fear-based motivation as the primary storytelling tool
- Anything that could cause nightmares — threat level stays age-appropriate
- Bullying played for laughs

### Humor Guidelines

**GOOD HUMOR:**
- Wordplay and puns (Fun-Gus, "fun gal," etc.)
- Gentle absurdity (mushrooms talking)
- Character-based humor (Spora's mistakes, Truff-Low's slowness)
- Tongue-in-cheek references adults catch but don't harm kids
- Visual gags

**AVOID:**
- Sarcasm kids won't understand
- Humor at characters' expense
- Jokes that require "getting it" to enjoy the story
- Anything that could be repeated inappropriately

### The "Two Levels" Approach

Content should work on two levels:

| Kid Level | Adult Level |
|-----------|-------------|
| Nice teacher helps them | "Wait, is she a faerie?" |
| Mushrooms talk! | "This is accurate mycology" |
| Fun-Gus is friendly | "This is well-designed pedagogy" |
| Trees share food | "Mycorrhizal nutrient exchange" |
| Pretty colors mean things | "Semiotic color language system" |
| Bad mushroom wants to control others | "That's literally how Cordyceps works" |
| Good bacteria helps the network | "Nitrogen-fixing rhizobia are essential to ecosystems" |
| The villain has a reason | "This is teaching moral nuance, not just good vs. evil" |

The adult layer adds depth WITHOUT:
- Changing the kid experience
- Adding inappropriate content
- Making kids feel they're missing something
- Creating "parents only" jokes

### Scientific Accuracy

**POLICY:** Real science, presented accessibly

- Don't dumb down — translate
- Don't invent fake science — simplify real science
- When in doubt, check sources
- Include "The Real Science" sections for those who want more

### Representation Guidelines

- Diverse characters (the girls, background characters)
- No stereotypes
- Girls are adventurers, scientists, heroes
- Adults are supportive, not dismissive
- Different ways of being smart (Spora learns differently than Fun-Gus teaches)

### Sensitivity Review Checklist

Before finalizing any content:
- [ ] Would I read this to a 4-year-old?
- [ ] Would a parent thank me for this content?
- [ ] Does it pass the "repeated by kids" test?
- [ ] Is the science accurate?
- [ ] Are all characters treated with dignity?
- [ ] Does it leave kids feeling good?
