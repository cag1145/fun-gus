EXPLAINER VIDEO: THE REAL SCIENCE OF SILLIUM
=============================================
Format: Animated explainer (1-2 minutes)
Status: Concept Phase

PURPOSE
-------
Connect the fictional fungal world to real scientific concepts.
Bridge entertainment and education.
Enhance viewer connection to characters by showing "this is real."

STRUCTURE
---------
1. Open with fictional scene (Fun-Gus sending a message)
2. Zoom out / transition to real science diagram
3. "In real life, this is called..."
4. Show real footage/images of fungal networks
5. Return to characters: "So when Fun-Gus glows, he's really..."

CONCEPTS TO COVER
-----------------
- Mycelium structure (the "threads")
- Mycorrhizal networks (fungi-root partnerships)
- Nutrient sharing (the "wood wide web")
- Chemical signaling between organisms
- Scale (how vast these networks really are)

DRAFT SCRIPT
------------
[To be developed]

VISUAL TRANSITION IDEAS
-----------------------
- Character glow → microscope view of real mycelium
- Sillium map → actual forest floor diagram
- Fun-Gus's cap → real mushroom cross-section
- Animated message pulse → scientific diagram of chemical signals

FACTS TO INCLUDE
----------------
- A single fungal network can span entire forests
- Trees share up to 40% of their carbon through fungal networks
- "Mother trees" send extra nutrients to seedlings
- Networks can transmit warning signals about insects/disease
- Fungi have existed for over 450 million years
- One tablespoon of forest soil contains miles of mycelium

TONE
----
- Wonder, not lecture
- "Isn't this amazing?" not "Here are the facts"
- Characters react with us
- Short, punchy, visual

CALL TO ACTION
--------------
End with: "Next time you're in a forest, remember—there's a whole
internet beneath your feet. And now you know who's running it."

[Show Fun-Gus waving]
