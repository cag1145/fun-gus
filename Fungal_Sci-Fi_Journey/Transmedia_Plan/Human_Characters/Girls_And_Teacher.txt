THE TWO GIRLS
=============

RELATIONSHIP: Best Friends
- Met through shared curiosity about nature
- Complement each other's personalities
- Brave together (braver than either alone)

AGE: 7-8 years old
- Old enough for some independence
- Young enough for wonder
- Relatable to target audience (4-9)

NAME STATUS: TBD
Name Options Being Considered:
- Maya, Myla, Mira
- Fern, Ivy, Hazel
- Luna, Willow, Sage
- Rue, Sylvie, Wren

SHARED TRAITS:
- Notice small things others miss
- Ask questions
- Respect nature
- Kind to each other and creatures they meet

INDIVIDUAL DIFFERENTIATION:

GIRL A (Working name: "Fern")
- Personality: Slightly more cautious, thoughtful
- Question style: "Why does it do that?"
- Notices: Details, patterns, small creatures
- Role in pair: The one who slows down to observe
- Sillium connection: Bonds with Spora (both learners)
- Visual distinction: TBD (maybe glasses? nature journal?)

GIRL B (Working name: "Ivy")
- Personality: Slightly more bold, adventurous
- Question style: "What if we tried this?"
- Notices: Paths, possibilities, what's ahead
- Role in pair: The one who takes the first step
- Sillium connection: Bonds with Fun-Gus (both guides)
- Visual distinction: TBD (maybe always slightly muddy? explorer energy)

VISUAL DESIGN DIRECTION:
- Practical outdoor clothing (NOT "cute" or "fancy")
- Rainboots or sturdy shoes
- Clothes that can get dirty
- Diverse representation (ethnicity to be determined)
- Hair: Practical for outdoors (ponytails, braids, short cuts)
- NO princess aesthetic — these are explorers
- Should look like real kids, not "TV kids"

WHAT THEY MIGHT CARRY:
- Small backpacks
- Nature journal and pencil (Fern?)
- Magnifying glass (Ivy?)
- Snacks (realistic kid detail)
- Keep it minimal and purposeful

THE TEACHER — SECRET FAERIE
===========================

SURFACE IDENTITY
Name Status: TBD
Name Options (folklore/nature appropriate):
- Ms. Rowan (rowan tree = protection in folklore)
- Ms. Hawthorn (haerie tree, threshold guardian)
- Ms. Meadows (gentle, pastoral)
- Ms. Moss (connected to forest floor)
- Ms. Alder (water-loving tree, transformation)
- Ms. Briar (wild, protective)

Surface Role: Nature educator, field trip leader
Apparent Age: Late 20s to early 40s
Job: Elementary school teacher or nature program educator

TRUE NATURE: FAERIE

Faerie Type: Aos Sí / Folk Faerie (Celtic/European folklore tradition)

NOT:
- Tolkien elf (tall, ethereal, warrior)
- Disney fairy (tiny, winged, sparkly)
- Tinkerbell type (cute, mischievous)
- Any "high fantasy" interpretation

YES:
- Appears fully human
- Subtly "other" — something slightly off but you can't name it
- Ancient being in human form
- Connected to liminal spaces and natural systems
- Operates on different timescales
- Sees what humans cannot

WHY SHE'S HERE:
- Faeries have always tended the balance of natural systems
- She's watched humans lose connection to nature over centuries
- Children still have the capacity for wonder; adults have forgotten
- She's "planting seeds" — creating humans who understand
- She and Fun-Gus are old allies

HOW SHE CHOSE THESE GIRLS:
- They notice things
- They ask the right questions
- They're kind to small creatures
- They're ready to see

PERSONALITY:
- Warm and encouraging
- Asks questions instead of giving answers
- Never seems surprised by anything
- Speaks with unusual phrasing sometimes ("the old ways")
- Patient beyond normal human patience
- Eyes that seem to know more than she says

VISUAL DESIGN DIRECTION:
- Appears human in every way
- Practical outdoor clothing, earth tones
- Slightly old-fashioned touches (hard to pin down era)
- Comfortable, worn-in boots (walked many miles)
- Perhaps a distinctive piece (unusual brooch? woven bracelet?)
- Hair: Natural, practical, maybe with leaves in it she doesn't notice
- Warm smile that reaches her eyes
- Ageless quality — could be 30 or 300

HINT SYSTEM (For Adult Readers)

These hints should be VISUAL and NARRATIVE, never stated:

Visual Hints:
- Animals approach her without fear
- Plants seem to lean toward her
- She's never wet when it rains (or doesn't notice)
- Insects don't bother her
- The fairy ring glows brighter near her
- She casts a slightly unusual shadow (maybe?)
- Her brooch/jewelry has ancient-looking designs

Behavioral Hints:
- She hums melodies no one recognizes
- She knows exactly where the fairy ring is (without looking)
- She references "the old ways" casually
- When asked how long she's been teaching: "Oh, longer than you'd think"
- She's never lost in the forest
- She knows the girls' questions before they ask
- Fun-Gus greets her like an old friend

Background Hints (for attentive readers):
- "Old Mrs. [Someone] remembers her from when SHE was a girl"
- Historical photos in the school with someone who looks like her
- She references events from long ago as if she was there

THE RULE: Never confirmed. Only wondered.

DOES SHE SHRINK?

Options to consider:

A) Yes, she shrinks with the girls
- Pros: She can interact with Fun-Gus, shared adventure
- Cons: Might overshadow girls as protagonists

B) No, she waits above ground
- Pros: Girls have independence, she's the threshold guardian
- Cons: Can't see her with Fun-Gus (which would be delightful)

C) She shrinks but stays at the entry chamber
- Pros: Brief interaction with Fun-Gus, then girls explore freely
- Cons: Artificial limitation

D) She "doesn't need to shrink" (faerie hint)
- Pros: Strong hint she's not human
- Cons: Might be too obvious for kid readers

CURRENT LEANING: Option C or D — needs decision

RELATIONSHIP WITH FUN-GUS:
- They know each other (old friends, likely centuries)
- Mutual respect and affection
- She trusts him with "her" children
- He trusts her to bring the right ones
- Brief exchanges that hint at long history
- Fun-Gus might call her something other than "Ms. [Name]"
